## Story Synopsis

The 1893 World's Fair pavilions were not temporary exhibition halls. They were the REAL White City — a Tartarian district disguised as a "fair" by Reset operatives who scheduled its demolition before the plaster was even dry. Beneath the official record of a grand exhibition lies a darker truth: the pavilions were inventoried, documented, and systematically destroyed to erase evidence of an advanced civilization hiding in plain sight.

Five buried pavilions survive beneath the mudline. Restoring them reveals demolition orders signed by the Parasite Cabal — and the original World's Fair schedule, which was not a celebration of progress but a **farewell inventory** of wonders about to be wiped from memory. Fountain holograms show the real crowds: humans and giants walking together, children on 12-foot shoulders, airships docked at pavilion spires.

**Thematic Core:** Erasure, deception, and the courage of being remembered. What happens when a civilization's death is disguised as entertainment?

## Main Quest Chain

### Quest 1: The Plaster Lie
**Trigger:** 30% grid resonance achieved. White City resonance reveals subsurface signatures.
**Objective:** Investigate anomalous Aether readings beneath the White City fairgrounds.

- Scanning reveals 5 massive structures beneath an 8m mud layer — too deep for the initial Moon 1 excavation
- Surface evidence: fragments of "plaster" facades are actually resonance-conducting ceramic — advanced material disguised as cheap construction
- First excavation reveals Pavilion 1: the Hall of Frequencies — a concert hall with integrated pipe organ and fountain array, buried intact

> **Thorne:** "Plaster? That's resonance ceramic. Grade-four crystalline matrix. We used it in airship hulls. That's not 'temporary construction' — that's mil-spec."
> **Milo:** "So the whole 'World's Fair' thing was…"
> **Thorne:** "A garage sale. They told the world it was a party while they packed up the house."

**Completion:** First pavilion excavated. Fair schedule fragment recovered.

### Quest 2: The Five Pavilions
**Trigger:** Pavilion 1 excavated.
**Objective:** Locate and excavate all 5 buried pavilions.

Each pavilion reveals a different aspect of the Tartarian White City:

| # | Pavilion | Function | Key Discovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hall of Frequencies | Concert & resonance research | Organ tuning logs matching Zereth's calibration signature |
| 2 | Court of Stars | Astronomical observatory & navigation center | Star charts showing airship routes across 6 continents |
| 3 | Pavilion of Waters | Hydraulic engineering & fountain design | Aquifer connection blueprints (feeds Moon 11) |
| 4 | The Giant's Gallery | Diplomatic meeting hall (human/giant dual-scale) | First evidence of formal human-giant governance |

- Each excavation is a multi-step dig with scanning, cutting, purifying, and finally activating the pavilion's dormant systems
- Pavilions connect in sequence — restoring one provides power/resonance to help excavate the next
- Demolition orders recovered from each pavilion, progressively revealing the Parasite Cabal's chain of command

**Completion:** All 5 pavilions excavated and connected. The White City echo district is operational.

### Quest 3: The Farewell Inventory
**Trigger:** All 5 pavilions active.
**Objective:** Assemble the complete World's Fair schedule from fragments found in each pavilion.

- 25 schedule fragments scattered across the 5 pavilions
- Reassembled, the schedule reveals the "fair" was organized into two tracks:
  - **Public Track:** "Exotic exhibits" shown to visitors — displays stripped of context, wonders reduced to curiosities
  - **Hidden Track:** Simultaneous inventory and packing of genuine Tartarian technology for distribution to Cabal members
- The Cabal divided the spoils: one took the fountain hydraulics, another the resonance instruments, the third the astronomical equipment
- Names on the distribution ledgers match Reset agent signatures found in Moon 2 and Moon 10

> **Lirael (reading the hidden track, hands shaking):** "They divided us like furniture. This column says 'AUCTION LOT 47 — Pipe organ, 61 registers, operational, approximate value: immeasurable. Status: Demolition scheduled for October 15.'"
> **Lirael:** "They knew the value. They destroyed it BECAUSE they knew."

**Completion:** Full schedule assembled. Demolition Phantom triggered.

### Quest 4: Aurora Hologram Activation
**Trigger:** Schedule assembled and read.
**Objective:** Activate the fountain hologram system to witness the true World's Fair.

- The 5 pavilion fountains, when synchronized, spray ionized mist that forms holographic images of the fair as it truly was
- Not a recording — a RESONANCE MEMORY embedded in the water itself
- The hologram shows:
  - Crowds of mixed-height people — humans and giants together, casually, familiarly
  - Children riding on giant shoulders to see over parade crowds
  - Airships docked at pavilion spires — passengers disembarking with luggage, not gawking like tourists
  - A pipe organ performance that fills the entire fairground with golden light
  - The LAST DAY: demolition crews arriving while families still celebrate, children pulled away crying, giants standing silently as their buildings are wired with explosives

> **Milo (watching the last-day hologram):** "They blew it up while kids were still playing. What kind of—" *(trails off, unable to finish)*

**Completion:** Hologram sequence witnessed. Zone RS boost applied to all restored pavilions.

### Quest 5: The Demolition Phantom
**Trigger:** Hologram sequence complete.

> **Thorne (grim):** "That's the foreman. The man they hired to destroy the White City. His hate was so efficient it left an imprint."

## Side Quests

### Side Quest A: The Guide Book
Reconstruct the souvenir guide book from fragments. 12 pages, each containing a hand-drawn illustration of a pavilion with annotations in Old Tartarian. Completing the guide unlocks a decorative "original guide" skin for the player's journal.

### Side Quest B: Giant's Gallery Diplomacy
The Giant's Gallery contains records of formal treaties between human and giant councils. 4 treaty documents detail mutual obligations, shared resource management, and a "resonance covenant" — a legal framework ensuring both species maintained harmonic balance. Rewards: permanent diplomacy-themed RS buff.

### Side Quest C: Children's Kingdom Revival
The education center still has functional teaching tools. Restore 6 interactive resonance demonstrations. Each teaches the player a new resonance technique AND becomes a functional exhibit that NPC children visit (permanent ambient content).

### Side Quest D: Thorne's Memory Lane
Thorne recognizes specific pavilions — he visited the star-chart room before the Flood. Guide him through a personal archaeology: recovering his original navigation tools from a locked case. Deeply emotional companion quest.

## New Zone: World's Fair Echo Districts

### Layout
5 interconnected pavilion zones surrounding the White City central spire (from base campaign):

```
           [Court of Stars]
               ╱     ╲
    [Hall of Freq.]   [Pavilion of Waters]
               │   ✦   │
    [Giant's Gallery]  [Children's Kingdom]
```

Center marker (✦) = Central Fountain Hub (hologram projector, fast-travel point)

### Environmental Properties
- **Architecture:** Beaux-Arts + Tartarian — white marble facades with crystalline internal structure, copper-inlaid resonance channels visible through translucent walls
- **Aether Density:** High (3× surface normal) — pavilions amplify each other when connected
- **Acoustics:** Each pavilion has distinct acoustic character — the Hall of Frequencies has concert-hall reverb (3.2s RT60), the Children's Kingdom has warm intimate acoustics (0.8s RT60)
- **Special:** Fountain hologram system available on demand after Quest 4 completion

### Visual Identity
The Echo District is the most visually striking zone in the game for sheer architectural density. Every sight line is filled with impossible beauty: colonnades stretching hundreds of meters, domes that catch aurora light, sculptural details at both human and giant scale coexisting naturally.

## Core Mechanic: Pavilion Restoration Chains

### Description
Pavilions connect through resonance links. Restoring one provides power/resources to adjacent pavilions, creating chain effects:

### Chain Effects
| Connected Count | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 pavilion | Basic illumination, Aether harvesting point |
| 2 connected | Resonance amplification (+15% RS in zone) |
| 3 connected | Fountain synchronization (hologram fragments appear) |
| 4 connected | Inter-pavilion fast travel, ambient NPC population |
| 5 connected (all) | Full hologram system, planetary bell-tower chorus boost |

### Restoration Process (per pavilion)
1. **Excavation:** Remove mud layers (scanning + precision cutting)
2. **Structural Assessment:** Identify damaged resonance conduits (Lirael-assisted frequency analysis)
3. **Core Repair:** Replace/restore crystalline resonance nodes (crafting + mini-game)
4. **Activation:** Tune the pavilion's master frequency (tuning fork alignment)
5. **Connection:** Link to adjacent restored pavilions (resonance bridge establishment)

### Long-Term Benefits
Fully connected pavilion network provides:
- 25% Aether harvesting boost to all White City zones
- Floating platform generation at pavilion spires (airship dock expansion)
- Resonance amplification that feeds the Moon 12 bell-tower synchronization

## New Enemies

### Plaster Wraiths
- **Description:** Humanoid shapes formed from the demolished pavilions' facade fragments — hollow, crumbling, carrying ghostly demolition tools
- **Behavior:** Patrol former pavilion perimeters, aggressive when player begins restoration, attempt to re-bury excavated areas
- **Weakness:** Sustained resonance tone (3 seconds of directed frequency) dissolves their structural bonds
- **Loot:** Resonance ceramic fragments (crafting material, building material)

### Archive Feeders
- **Description:** Worm-like corruption entities that consume stone tablets and lore fragments
- **Behavior:** Spawn near unprotected lore items, retreat to mud walls when confronted
- **Weakness:** Lirael's singing immobilizes them; player finishes with resonance pulse
- **Loot:** Preserved lore fragment (bonus XP for each recovery)

### Demolition Drones
- **Description:** Mechanical entities resembling miniature cranes and wrecking balls — echoes of the actual demolition machinery
- **Behavior:** Target restored structures specifically — will bypass the player to damage buildings
- **Weakness:** Mercury-coated resonance attacks (DLC 1 mercury flask crafting prerequisite)
- **Priority:** Must be intercepted before they reach restored structures — defense-oriented combat

## Boss: The Demolition Phantom

**Location:** Central Fountain Hub, after full hologram activation
**Description:** 30-foot manifestation of concentrated destruction trauma. Wears a top hat. Carries a crystal detonator. Its body is constructed from shattered facades, bent structural steel, and smoke that never dissipates.

### Phases

**Phase 1 — The Foreman:**
- Attacks: commands Demolition Drones (summons 3 waves of 4), hurls explosive debris, ground-slam shockwaves
- Mechanic: explosives are timed (3-second fuse) — player can REDIRECT them into the Phantom using resonance kinesis
- Dialogue: the Phantom speaks in fragments of demolition orders — "Lot 47, condemned." "Schedule ahead of time. Remove evidence."

**Phase 2 — The Explosion:**
- At 50%: the Phantom detonates itself, creating a smoke-filled arena
- Limited visibility — player relies on Aether scanner to track the Phantom's resonance signature
- The Phantom attacks from the smoke — ambush strikes, reappearances at random positions
- Counter: activate fountain sprays to clear smoke sections, cornering the Phantom progressively

**Phase 3 — The Memory:**
- At 25%: the Phantom stops fighting. Instead, it replays the destruction in real-time around the player — buildings collapsing, people fleeing, giants standing helplessly.
- NOT a combat phase. A CHOICE phase.
- Option A: Destroy the Phantom with a resonance overload (quick, violent, ends the memory)
- Option B: Counter-project the fountain holograms (slow, emotional, the Phantom sees what the pavilions BECAME when restored — and dissolves voluntarily)
- Option B rewards more RS, a unique lore entry, and an emotional companion dialogue beat

> **Phantom (if shown the restoration, voice thick with confusion):** "This was… supposed to stay buried. They said nobody would remember."
> **Lirael:** "We remember. We always remember."

## Lore Discoveries

### Discovery 1: The Plaster Deception
The "plaster" facades that history remembers were actually the LOWEST-QUALITY component of real buildings. The interior structure — resonance ceramic, crystalline grid, giant-scale engineering — was the genuine article. The Cabal ordered cheap plaster skins applied to the exterior specifically so that photographs would show "temporary construction" for the historical record.

### Discovery 2: The Farewell Inventory
The hidden track of the Fair schedule reveals a systematic cataloguing of Tartarian technology before destruction. Every major invention, every resonance device, every fountain mechanism was documented, photographed, and either distributed to Cabal warehouses or marked for demolition. This was not destruction through ignorance — it was asset stripping.

### Discovery 3: Human-Giant Governance
Treaty documents from the Giant's Gallery show that the Tartarian Empire was co-governed by human and giant councils. Decisions required consensus across both species. The Flood and Reset specifically targeted this governance structure — separating the species was the first objective, not a side effect.

### Discovery 4: The Guide's Message
Maeve's tour scripts contain coded messages in Old Tartarian — hidden instructions for future discoverers. She was PLANTING the evidence that would eventually be used to expose the deception. She knew the Fair was a farewell, and she ensured the farewell would speak.
