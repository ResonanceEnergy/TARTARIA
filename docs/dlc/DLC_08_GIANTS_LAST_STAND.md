## Story Synopsis

Korath has been an echo — a consciousness preserved in resonance fields — since the beginning. Powerful. Wise. Incorporeal. But echoes fade. And Korath is fading faster than any other because he's been pouring his remaining energy into the restoration grid. He estimates he has two Moons left before he dissipates entirely.

There is one hope: the Giant Stasis Vaults. Deep beneath mountain ranges, the giants built preservation chambers — not for artifacts, but for THEMSELVES. Living giants, hundreds of them, who entered voluntary stasis during the Mud Flood's first wave, trusting that future generations would wake them when the dissonance passed.

If Korath can reach a vault, if a living giant still breathes inside, and if the awakening protocol can be completed — Korath might transfer his echo into a body. Or a living giant might take his place in the grid. Or both might fail, and Korath will spend his final energy supercharging the grid in one devastating sacrifice.

**Thematic Core:** The difference between sacrifice and self-destruction is whether someone is there to receive what you give.

## Main Quest Chain

### Quest 1: The Fading
**Objective:** Confront Korath's deterioration. Find the Giant Stasis Vaults.

- Korath's echo has been flickering. Dialogue options reveal he's known for Moons — hiding it, giving his energy to the grid
- The conversation where he admits it is the most vulnerable Korath has ever been:

> **Korath (quietly, trying to sound matter-of-fact):** "I have... a confession. I've been transferring my resonance into the grid. Not because I'm noble. Because I'm terrified of fading without purpose. I have perhaps forty days. If I stop now, perhaps sixty. But the grid would lose ground."
> **Lirael:** "You could have told us."
> **Korath:** "And what? Made you carry the weight of watching me die? I've been building things my entire existence. Let me build this too."

- Thorne's fleet navigation records (DLC 5) contain vault coordinates — 17 locations across four continents
- Only 3 vaults show active resonance signatures. The rest are dead.

### Quest 2: Vault Alpha — The Sleepers
**Objective:** Reach and open the first active vault. Discover what's inside.

- Vault Alpha: beneath the Ural Mountains, 800 feet below the surface
- Entry: a massive circular door, 60 feet in diameter, sealed with a harmonic lock requiring 12 simultaneous frequencies (the full chromatic scale in 432 Hz base)
- Inside: a cathedral-sized chamber. 47 stasis pods. 46 are dark — power failed centuries ago. One still glows.
- The occupant: a female giant named Kaida. 30 feet tall. Alive. Breathing once every 72 hours. Her resonance signature is strong but her stasis is degrading.
- The pod cannot be opened with current technology. It requires an awakening protocol stored in a separate vault.

### Quest 3: Vault Beta — The Protocol
**Objective:** Retrieve the awakening protocol from the second active vault.

- Vault Beta: beneath the Atlas Mountains, North Africa
- This vault is not a stasis chamber — it's an ARCHIVE. The giants' backup plan documentation.
- The awakening protocol is a 7-step harmonic sequence that must be performed perfectly
- Also found: records of WHO entered stasis and WHY. Not all giants agreed to sleep. Some argued for fighting. Some for fleeing (the Armada — DLC 5). Some for stasis. The debate was bitter. Families split.
- Kaida's entry: she was a Calibration Engineer — the same role as Cassian's ancestor (DLC 7). She maintained the guardian giants' harmonic signatures. She entered stasis willingly, leaving a message: "I'll tune them again when I wake. However long it takes."

### Quest 4: Vault Gamma — The Brother
**Objective:** Investigate the third active vault. Discover Maelix.

- Vault Gamma: beneath the Canadian Shield, North America
- This vault should contain one sleeping giant. Instead: the pod is OPEN. Empty. Recently.
- Evidence of a giant moving through the vault — footprints, displaced rock, a resonance trail leading OUT
- Following the trail: a living giant, 35 feet tall, male, confused, angry, AWAKE
- This is Maelix. Korath's brother. Eryx's brother. The third of the builder trio.
- Maelix's stasis failed naturally — he's been awake for approximately 8 years, wandering underground, confused, terrified, producing dissonance involuntarily because his resonance is out of tune after centuries of sleep

> **Korath (barely able to speak):** "Maelix. Brother. You're... you're ALIVE. Not an echo. Not a memory. Alive."
> **Maelix (staring at the echo, uncomprehending):** "Korath? Why are you made of light? Where is your body? WHERE IS YOUR BODY, KORATH?"

### Quest 5: The Sacrifice
**Objective:** Choose how to spend Korath's remaining energy.

The player must decide — informed by everything they've learned across 8 Moons and 7 DLCs:

**Option A: Awaken Kaida**
- Use Korath's remaining energy + the awakening protocol to revive Kaida
- Kaida awakens. Korath fades. But his knowledge transfers to her in the final harmonic exchange.
- Kaida becomes the new giant companion — carrying Korath's wisdom in a living body
- The grid loses Korath's active support but gains a LIVING giant calibrator

**Option B: Stabilize Maelix**
- Use Korath's energy to re-tune Maelix, curing his dissonance
- Maelix stabilizes. Korath fades. Maelix takes up his brother's work.
- Maelix is raw strength — less wise than Korath but physically capable of things an echo never could be
- The grid gains a giant who can PHYSICALLY rebuild structures

**Option C: Supercharge the Grid (Korath's Choice)**
- Korath overrides the player. He pours ALL his remaining energy into the global resonance grid.
- He doesn't awaken anyone. He doesn't save himself.
- The grid's power DOUBLES. Half the restoration work for the remaining Moons is completed instantly.
- Korath is gone. Truly gone. The most devastating emotional moment in the game.

> **Korath (if Option C, his final words):** "Don't mourn me. I was never here to stay. I was here to build a bridge — and you're standing on it. Now WALK."

## Side Quests

### Side Quest A: The 46
The 46 dead stasis pods in Vault Alpha each contain a giant who died in their sleep. Scanning each one reveals their name, their specialty, and a fragment of the last thought in their consciousness. Completing all 46 scans creates a memorial wall — the most somber, respectful collectible in the game.

### Side Quest B: Maelix's 8 Years
Traces of Maelix's 8 years underground: carved messages in tunnel walls (desperate, then resigned, then curious), improvised tools, a shelter built in an enormous cavern. The player follows his journey from terror to survival to tentative exploration of the modern world from beneath it.

### Side Quest C: The Giant's Debate
Archives in Vault Beta contain recorded arguments between giant factions: Fight, Flee, or Sleep. 6 recordings that humanize the giants as a people with disagreements, fears, and family conflicts. The hawks wanted to confront the dissonance source directly. The doves wanted to evacuate. The sleepers believed future generations would solve what they couldn't.

### Side Quest D: Korath's Regret
In a private section of Vault Beta, sealed with Korath's personal harmonic: a message he left before becoming an echo. He didn't die in the Flood. He CHOSE to become an echo — transferring his consciousness into the grid deliberately so he could guide future restorers. He gave up his body. His message: "If anyone finds this: I'm sorry I couldn't be patient enough to sleep. I'm sorry I had to keep watching. I'm sorry I couldn't let go."

## New Zone: Giant Stasis Vaults

### Vault Architecture
The vaults are the most impressive enclosed spaces in the game — designed for beings 20-40 feet tall.

| Feature | Scale | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Corridors | 50 ft ceiling | Geometric precision cut from bedrock |
| Stasis Chamber | 200 ft dome | Central pod array in concentric rings |
| Archive Hall | 100 ft × 400 ft | Crystal storage shelves holding harmonic recordings |
| Emergency Exit | 80 ft diameter tunnel | Spiral ascent to surface (Maelix's escape route) |

### Environmental Storytelling
- Pod failure decay: the 46 dead pods show stages of degradation — some serene, some showing evidence of brief awakening before death
- Maelix's path: scratches on walls at 30-foot height, displaced boulders, resonance burns from involuntary emissions
- Scale contrast: the player is TINY in these spaces. Giant-mode barely reaches half the ceiling height.

### Vault Network Map
17 vault locations (3 active, 14 dead) connected by a deep-earth resonance transit system — the giant equivalent of a subway, using natural ley-line channels.

## Core Mechanic: Awaken / Recruit Living Giants

### Awakening Protocol (7 Steps)
| Step | Requirement | Challenge |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Identification | Scan pod for occupant identity | Technical |
| 2. Harmonic Assessment | Match the giant's personal frequency | Puzzle |
| 3. Stasis Dissolution | Gradually reduce stasis field power | Timed |
| 4. Resonance Restoration | Re-tune the giant's degraded harmonics | Musical |
| 5. Neural Reactivation | Stimulate consciousness return (micro-giant mode) | Exploration |
| 6. Physical Stabilization | Giant-mode support during awakening tremors | Combat |
| 7. Identity Confirmation | Speak the giant's name at their native frequency | Emotional |

### Recruited Giant Capabilities
Living giants (Kaida or Maelix, depending on player choice) provide:
- **Construction:** Lift and place massive stone blocks (echoes of original building methods)
- **Combat:** Direct physical confrontation with corruption entities
- **Resonance:** Amplified tuning — a living giant's harmonic output equals 10 resonance towers
- **Morale:** NPC population happiness increases permanently with a visible giant protector

## New Enemies

### Vault Guardians (Corrupted)
- **Description:** Stasis vault defense systems that have decayed into hostile entities
- **Behavior:** Protect dead pods aggressively — interpreting the player as a threat to the sleepers
- **Weakness:** Playing the vault's identification harmonic (found in Vault Beta) deactivates them

### Deep Earth Crawlers
- **Description:** Corruption entities that have burrowed into the vault network through ley-line channels
- **Behavior:** Consume resonance infrastructure, weakening stasis fields
- **Weakness:** Resonance traps set in ley-line junctions

### The Mega-Titan
- **Description:** A massive corruption entity drawn to the vault cluster's resonance — 60 feet tall, crystallized dissonance
- **Behavior:** Siege-style assault on whichever vault the player is currently in
- **Weakness:** Coordinated defense — Maelix (if present) can physically grapple it while the player targets corruption nodes

## Boss: Maelix (Untuned)

Before Maelix can be recruited or stabilized, the player must survive his dissonant outbursts. He's not evil — he's SCARED. A confused giant producing involuntary dissonance earthquakes.

### Phase 1: Tremors
Maelix's presence causes environmental destruction — collapsing tunnels, resonance shockwaves. The player must navigate the destruction while approaching him.

### Phase 2: Communication
Maelix can't understand human speech — his auditory processing is damaged from stasis degradation. The player must communicate through RESONANCE — tuning fork patterns that spell out Tartarian words. Korath coaches from the sideline, translating.

### Phase 3: Calming
Once communication is established, Maelix panics about Korath's echo state. His distress causes a dissonance SURGE. The player must physically shield Korath's echo while conducting a calming harmonic — the lullaby that Eryx used to sing (learned in DLC 4).

### Resolution
Maelix stabilizes. He kneels — a 35-foot being, kneeling before a human, weeping.

> **Maelix (through resonance-speech, raw):** "Brothers. Both my brothers. One is light. One is stone. And I am here. Alone. In a body that forgot how to sing."

## Lore Discoveries

### Discovery 1: The Giants' Population
Before the Flood: approximately 2,000 giants globally. 200 entered stasis. 500 were aboard the Armada. The rest — 1,300 — were either killed or converted into guardian structures (like Eryx's star fort). The giants did not go extinct. They were CONVERTED.

### Discovery 2: Zereth's Vote
In the Great Debate recordings: Zereth — the name that keeps appearing — voted for NONE of the three options. His speech: "We should not fight, flee, or sleep. We should REMEMBER. The world will forget us. That is the true death. I will ensure they never forget." His plan was not recorded. Only the vote.

### Discovery 3: The Stasis Sabotage
46 of 47 pods failed in Vault Alpha. Analysis of the failures: they weren't random degradation. They were SEQUENTIAL — pods failed one after another over 50 years, as if someone was visiting periodically and deactivating them. Kaida's pod survived because her personal frequency was unique — the saboteur couldn't crack her harmonic lock.

### Discovery 4: Korath's Full Truth
Korath's sealed message (Side Quest D) reveals the final piece: he didn't just choose to become an echo. He was ASKED to. By Zereth. Zereth said: "Someone must stay awake. Someone must guide them back. Will you be that someone?" Korath said yes. He has been awake, alone, for 300 years. Like Thorne on the ship — but longer. Much longer.
