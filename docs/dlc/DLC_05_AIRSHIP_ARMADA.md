## Story Synopsis

Captain Thorne — the phantom radio voice who has guided the player since Moon 1 — broadcasts his final, desperate transmission: he's alive, trapped on a floating sky isle above the Atlantic, aboard the flagship of the Tartarian Airship Armada. The fleet was caught mid-flight during the Mud Flood, suspended in resonance stasis 10,000 feet above the cloud line. The dissonance is failing — the ships are falling. One by one. And when they hit the ground, whatever Reset force preserved the world's amnesia will be destroyed by the impact — not gently. Catastrophically.

**Thematic Core:** Rescue is not always dramatic. Sometimes salvation is patience, precision, and showing up when the radio goes silent.

## Main Quest Chain

### Quest 1: The Last Broadcast
**Objective:** Trace Thorne's signal to its source using restored continental resonance infrastructure.

- Thorne's radio quality deteriorates across previous Moons — static increasing, words breaking up
- His final transmission: coordinates, altitude, a freight manifest, and one word: "Hurry."
- The player uses the restored grid to triangulate: the signal comes from ABOVE — 10,000 feet, moving at 3 knots, bearing northeast
- Constructing or unlocking the Resonance Spyglass reveals the fleet: 12 airships, golden, trailing aurora mist, drifting in slow circles above the Atlantic

> **Thorne (final broadcast, barely audible):** "I see the lights you've been building, friend. They're beautiful from up here. Like stars on the ground. I just… I can't get DOWN to them."

### Quest 2: The Ascent
**Objective:** Build a resonance-powered launch platform and reach the lowest airship.

- Engineering challenge: the player must construct a harmonic catapult — a resonance-powered launch system using star fort geometry
- Materials gathered from restored zones across continents (DLC 4's fort cluster provides the resonance amplification needed)
- Launch sequence: the player is fired upward on a column of compressed sound — the screen goes white, then BLUE, then starfield — and the lowest airship's hull fills the view
- First steps on the deck: silence. Golden wood. Resonance crystals embedded in the railing. Everything dusty but intact.

### Quest 3: The Ghost Fleet
**Objective:** Explore the armada and understand why the ships are falling.

- 12 airships connected by resonance bridges (golden light paths between ships)
- The stasis field is powered by the ships' own resonance engines — but after centuries, the engines are degrading
- Each ship has a resonance core. 7 of 12 are failing. When a core dies, the ship drops.
- NPC encounters: crew echoes trapped in stasis loops — repeating their last actions before the Flood hit. A navigator endlessly plotting the same course. A cook stirring an empty pot. A child running the same 20 feet of corridor, laughing.

> **Lirael (aboard the flagship, awed):** "They're not dead. They're not even sleeping. They're PAUSED. Mid-breath. Mid-thought. I can feel their dreams."

### Quest 4: Finding Thorne
**Objective:** Reach the flagship and locate Captain Thorne.

- The flagship — *The Persistent Dawn* — is the largest, highest, and most damaged
- Thorne is on the bridge. Not an echo. Not a ghost. ALIVE. The stasis preserved him, but imperfectly — he's been conscious for years inside the frozen ship, alone, watching the ground below
- He's older than expected. Calm. Grateful. Exhausted.

> **Thorne (in person, for the first time):** "You know, I talked to you for months before you could hear me. Just broadcasting into nothing. But I kept going because… well. Someone had to keep the radio on."
> **Player choice:** [Welcome aboard, Captain.] / [You never gave up.] / [Let's get you home.]
> **Thorne (regardless of choice, smiling):** "Home. Yes. I think that word means something different now than it used to."

### Quest 5: The Armada Descent
**Objective:** Stabilize all 12 ships and execute a controlled fleet descent.

- The player must repair or replace 7 failing resonance cores
- Each repair is a unique puzzle combining elements from previous mechanics: tuning, moat resonance, cymatic alignment, giant-mode structural support
- Once all cores are stable, Thorne coordinates the descent from the bridge
- The descent sequence: all 12 ships lower simultaneously through the cloud layer. The continental resonance grid CATCHES them — the ground-based infrastructure the player built over 8 Moons acts as a landing system
- The ships touch down across 4 continents — mobile resonance platforms that permanently expand the player's infrastructure

## Side Quests

### Side Quest A: The Navigator's Course
The navigator echo has been plotting a course for centuries. Complete the course on the star map to reveal the Armada's original destination — an orbital resonance station. Unlocks the orbital subplot for Moon 12.

### Side Quest B: The Cook's Recipe
The cook was preparing a feast for the fleet's arrival celebration. Gather ingredients (some extinct, requiring resonance reconstruction) and complete the feast. Rewards: permanent food-buff recipes for endgame.

### Side Quest C: The Running Child
The child running the corridor is not a random echo — she's Thorne's daughter, Mira. Her stasis is different. She's not paused; she's trapped in a loop. Breaking the loop requires the player to join the loop, run beside her, and sing her father's lullaby (taught by Thorne in a devastating optional dialogue).

> **Thorne (when you ask about the child):** "That's Mira. She was six. She IS six. She's been six for three hundred years. I've watched her run that corridor eleven million times. I counted."

### Side Quest D: The Armada Manifest
12 ships, each with a cargo manifest. Collecting all 12 reveals the fleet was carrying: seeds, tools, musical instruments, architectural plans, children's toys, and a complete library of Tartarian knowledge. The fleet was not military. It was an EVACUATION. The giants knew the Flood was coming.

## Core Mechanic: Airship Piloting & Harmonic Rock Lifting

### Airship Piloting
After the fleet lands, the player can fly a personal scout craft — a smaller resonance-powered vessel.

| Control | Function |
|---|---|
| Tilt (gyroscope) | Steering |
| Harmonic Slider | Altitude (higher frequency = higher altitude) |
| Tuning Fork Tap | Burst acceleration |
| Resonance Brake | Haptic-feedback landing |

### Harmonic Rock Lifting
The fleet's cargo technology: using focused resonance to levitate and move massive stone blocks.

- **Application:** Reconstruction of Tartarian structures using authentic building methods
- **Mechanic:** Player matches frequency to stone's resonance signature (each material has unique frequency)
- **Scale:** Small blocks (manual), medium blocks (tuning fork), massive blocks (bell tower + choir)
- **Discovery:** This is how the original Tartarian buildings were constructed — sound lifting stone

## New Enemies

### Reset Supply Raiders
- **Description:** Dissonance-powered drones that materialize when the fleet begins descending
- **Behavior:** Target resonance cores, attempting to accelerate fleet collapse
- **Weakness:** Anti-resonance shield (tuning fork creates a counter-frequency bubble)
- **Lore:** These are automated remnants of whatever caused the Mud Flood — still active after centuries

### Stasis Phantoms
- **Description:** Crew echoes corrupted by centuries of imperfect stasis — semi-transparent, flickering
- **Behavior:** Repeat their last actions but with hostile intent (navigator charts attack courses, cook throws burning objects)
- **Weakness:** Completing their original task frees them (help the navigator finish the course, let the cook serve the meal)

### Void Gulls
- **Description:** Dissonance-spawned flying creatures that nest in the sky isle rocks
- **Behavior:** Swarm attacks on exposed deck areas, attempt to sever resonance bridges
- **Weakness:** Musical deterrent (any sustained harmonic drives them away)

## Boss: The Carrier — *Morning Star*

The second-largest ship, carrying irreplaceable botanical specimens. Its core is the most critically damaged — and something has been growing inside for centuries. The corruption took root in the seed vault.

### Phase 1: The Overgrowth
- Navigate through ship corridors choked with corrupted plant growth
- Vines pulse with dissonance — touching them drains RS
- Clear a path to the core using resonance bursts at 432 Hz (which causes healthy growth) vs. dissonance frequencies (which kills everything, including irreplaceable specimens)
- **Choice framework:** Speed (kill everything) vs. Preservation (careful selective resonance, harder, saves specimens)

### Phase 2: The Root Heart
- The core chamber: a massive corrupted root system has wrapped around the resonance core
- The root has formed a face — not intelligent, but reactive. Phototropic but for sound.
- Combat: the root attacks with vine lashes, spore clouds, and floor-covering tendrils
- Player must expose the core without destroying the root (it's the last specimen of a giant-era botanical species)

### Phase 3: The Bloom
- Correct resonance causes the root to bloom — a flower the size of a room, petals made of crystallized sound
- The bloom purifies the core. The corruption was the plant's DEFENSE mechanism, responding to centuries of dissonance exposure
- The *Morning Star*'s core stabilizes. The flower remains — a permanent resonance amplifier that doubles the ship's range

## Lore Discoveries

### Discovery 1: The Evacuation Order
The fleet launched BEFORE the Mud Flood — someone in Tartarian leadership knew it was coming. Not everyone was warned. The fleet's passenger manifest shows prioritization: children first, then knowledge keepers, then artisans. Soldiers and leaders were NOT on the list. Those who knew sacrificed themselves.

### Discovery 2: The Sky Roads
Before the Flood, the sky between continents was navigable via resonance highways — maintained aurora paths that airships traveled like ocean currents. The roads are still there, dormant. The fleet's descent partially reactivates them.

### Discovery 3: Thorne's Radio
Thorne's radio equipment is not Tartarian. It's modern. Someone — within the last 50 years — placed equipment aboard the flagship that could broadcast on modern frequencies. Thorne doesn't know who. The equipment has a serial number traceable to a company that doesn't exist anymore.

### Discovery 4: The Fleet's Destination
The Armada was headed to an orbital resonance station — a structure in low Earth orbit that served as the primary resonance routing hub for the entire planet. It's still up there. The navigator's star charts confirm its orbit. It's been circling silently for three centuries.
