## 1. Design Philosophy

Aether is not magic — it is physics. The *right* physics. The physics the Reset buried.

In TARTARIA WORLD OF WONDER, the Aether energy system is:

- **The core mechanic** — every building, combat action, and progression milestone revolves around Aether
- **Visual & audible** — players *see* golden ley lines light up and *hear* buildings hum when tuned
- **Rewarding harmony** — perfect alignment produces exponential returns; sloppy construction produces diminishing returns and attracts danger
- **Session-friendly** — meaningful harvesting in 2–3 minutes per session
- **The narrative spine** — the grid's awakening IS the story; every lit ley line is a chapter

The system should feel like conducting an orchestra — not managing a spreadsheet. If a player ever thinks about math instead of music, we've failed.

## 2. Core Aether Concepts

### The Aether Field
A global, invisible scalar field that constantly replenishes from the ionosphere. On-screen representation:
- **Faint shimmer** in the air (parallax particle layer)
- **Stronger at night** (blue-violet glow increases after dusk)
- **Concentrated over ley lines** (visible golden veins in the terrain)
- **Absent in corrupted zones** (dead gray patches, no shimmer)

### Aether as Currency
Aether is the **sole resource currency** in the game:
- No gold, no gems, no secondary currencies
- Harvested from restored buildings
- Spent on construction, giant mode, combat abilities, and airship fuel
- Surplus flows into the global grid, lighting ley lines and unlocking new zones
- The economy is ecological — overuse creates corruption; balanced flow triggers golden-age booms

### Aether States

| State | Description | Visual |
|---|---|---|
| **Dormant** | Buried/blocked by mud or dissonance | No glow, gray tones |
| **Flowing** | Active in tuned structures and ley lines | Golden veins, shimmer |
| **Surplus** | Excess beyond local need, flowing outward | Bright arcs between buildings |
| **Overloaded** | Past capacity — spectacular but risky | Chain lightning, floating objects |
| **Corrupted** | Inverted by dissonance crystals or golems | Black sludge veins, crackling red |

## 4. Resonance Coefficient Formula

Every restored structure has a live **Resonance Score (RS)** from 0 to 100:

```
RS = (GA × 0.40) + (FT × 0.30) + (MP × 0.15) + (GB × 0.15)
```

### Components

| Component | Symbol | Range | How to Maximize |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Geometry Alignment** | GA | 0–100 | Use golden-ratio snap templates; align to ley-line grid |
| **Frequency Tune** | FT | 0–100 | Perfect score in cymatic/organ mini-game |
| **Material Purity** | MP | 0–100 | Use premium excavated materials (deeper = purer) |
| **Grid Bonus** | GB | 0–100 | Connect more ley lines; cluster buildings in sacred formations |

### Score Thresholds

| RS Range | Status | Visual | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–24 | **Dead** | No glow, crumbling | No Aether output; attracts golems |
| 25–49 | **Flickering** | Intermittent dim glow | Minimal 3-band output; occasional dissonance |
| 50–74 | **Stable** | Steady golden veins | Full 3-band, partial 6-band output |
| 75–89 | **Harmonic** | Bright pulsing glow + hum | Full 3+6 band, partial 9-band |
| 90–99 | **Resonant** | Brilliant aurora + music | Full all-band output; attracts Echo NPCs |
| 100 | **Perfect Resonance** | Transcendent glow + levitation | Max output + 2× multiplier; building "sings" |

## 5. Harvesting & Alignment Loop

Players place or repair three core structural components on every building:

### A. Antenna Component (Spires/Finials)
- **Function:** Pull raw Aether down from the ionosphere
- **Interaction:** Tap to place → auto-aligns to nearest ley-line direction
- **Visual:** Blue-white sparks climb the spire like Tesla coils; crackling intensifies at night
- **Upgrade Path:** Copper → Silver → Gold → Mercury-ball terminal

### B. Resonator Component (Domes/Vaults)
- **Function:** Amplify and convert raw Aether into usable band energy
- **Interaction:** Swipe to shape → snap to golden-ratio curve guides
- **Visual:** Interior frescoes glow; rose windows project cymatic patterns onto floors
- **Upgrade Path:** Small ribbed → Large ribbed → Onion → Fractal nested

### C. Grounding Component (Star-Fort Bastion/Moat/Crystal Array)
- **Function:** Complete the circuit; push energy into the ley-line grid
- **Interaction:** Drag moat channels → fill with conductive water puzzle
- **Visual:** Bastions crackle with ground discharge; moats glow with conducted Aether
- **Upgrade Path:** Dry bastion → Shallow moat → Deep conductive moat → Crystal-lined channel

## 6. Tuning Mini-Games

### 6.1 Pipe Organ Console (Cathedrals)

**How it Works:**
- Enter a restored cathedral → approach the massive pipe organ
- UI shows a circular dial divided into 9 segments (3 sets of 3-6-9)
- Tap keys in the correct 3-6-9 sequence as the harmonic "request" scrolls
- Timing matters — perfect beats produce cymatic water patterns on screen
- Escalating difficulty: simple 3-note in early game → complex 9-note cascading sequences in endgame

**Visual Feedback:**
- Correct notes: Visible sound waves ripple through the cathedral; baptismal water forms geometric patterns
- Perfect sequence: Rose windows project kaleidoscopic cymatic mandalas; building pulses with golden light
- Miss: Dissonant screech; gothic shadows flicker; -5 resonance on current building

**Touch Optimization (Mobile):**
- Bottom-screen key layout (thumb-friendly)
- Haptic feedback on correct hits (iPhone Taptic Engine pulse at 432 Hz timing)
- Short rounds (15–30 seconds per tune attempt)

### 6.2 Cymatic Garden Puzzle (Exterior Zones)

**How it Works:**
- Arrange crystal bowls and water channels on a sacred-geometry grid
- Drag crystals to nodes → match the standing-wave pattern to the target blueprint
- Target patterns are drawn from real cymatics (rosettes, hexagrams, 6-pointed stars)
- Water flows through channels dynamically; wave interference patterns form in real time

**Visual Feedback:**
- Correct placement: Water ripples form perfect geometric shapes; flowers bloom along channels
- Perfect match: Entire garden levitates briefly; ionized mist sprays golden aurora
- Miss: Muddy water; weeds grow; golems stir

### 6.3 Bell Tower Rhythm (Transmission Nodes)

**How it Works:**
- Tap-and-hold bell ropes at specific intervals
- Rhythm follows 3-beat, 6-beat, and 9-beat patterns
- Multiple bells = more complex polyrhythms
- Perfect performance sends scalar waves visibly across the sky to distant zones

**Visual Feedback:**
- Each bell ring: visible golden ripple expanding outward
- Perfect sequence: All bells ring simultaneously → continental aurora pulse
- Used for cross-zone activation: ringing a bell in Zone 4 can boost a structure in Zone 7

### 6.4 Harmonic Rock Cutting (Giant Mode)

**How it Works:**
- In giant mode, approach a rough megalith
- Hold resonance tool → swipe along desired cut line
- Frequency slider appears — adjust to match the stone's natural resonance (shown as vibrating particles)
- Perfect match: stone separates along crystalline planes with zero debris
- Imperfect: rough cut, lower material purity, wasted stone

**Touch Optimization:**
- Swipe-to-cut feels satisfying (like cutting fruit, but with *meaning*)
- Visual feedback: stone glows at cut line, then separates with a deeply satisfying "thunk"

## 7. Energy Storage & Distribution

### Local Aether Batteries
Every restored building contains an internal battery (capacity scales with RS):
- Stores surplus for on-demand use
- Drains slowly when building is unvisited (incentivizes return sessions)
- Visual: Glowing orb inside the structure; brighter = fuller

### Wireless Transmission
Power travels **instantly** along connected ley lines — no cables, no delay:
- Connect a new star fort → every linked building instantly gains +15% output
- Connect a new continent → network effect multiplier applies globally
- Visual: Golden veins pulse brighter when transmission occurs

### Global Aether Pool
Combined energy from all zones feeds a master pool:
- UI: Global meter at top of world map, showing total planetary resonance as percentage
- Threshold unlocks:
  - 25%: Airship network activated
  - 50%: Continental train routes open
  - 75%: Floating garden platforms spawn
  - 100%: True Timeline convergence accessible (endgame)

Star forts function as **network routers**:

- Routing bonus: +15% per connected fort in the chain

Cathedrals with fully tuned organs act as **signal amplifiers**:
- Increase transmission range of all buildings within their zone by 50%
- Stack with star-fort routing for continental coverage

## 10. Overload Mechanics

### Triggering Overload
Push a district past 100% Aether capacity by:
- Clustering too many high-RS buildings
- Playing a perfect multi-organ symphony during peak lunar phase
- Connecting a grid segment that suddenly channels massive surplus

### Overload Effects (Spectacular)
1. **Chain-Lightning Arcs:** Blue-white electricity arcs between all spires in the district (visual firework)
2. **Floating Gardens Rise:** Anti-grav bubbles lift garden platforms into the air
3. **City-Wide Harmonic Choir:** Procedural music swells as every building's hum combines into a symphony
4. **Aurora Explosion:** Sky fills with violet-gold aurora visible from neighboring zones
5. **Fountain Geysers:** Pure water fountains spray 30-foot ionized mist columns

### Overload Risks
- **Aether Storm:** Temporary 30-second weather event — high winds, crackling energy — damages low-RS structures
- **Elite Reset Agent Spawn:** Attracted by the "signal," a powerful enemy squad arrives to "suppress the anomaly"
- **Golem Awakening:** Dormant mud golems in adjacent zones stir and attack
- **Dissonance Cascade:** If any building in the cluster is below 50 RS, it cracks and must be repaired

### Risk vs. Reward
Deliberate overloads are an advanced strategy: massive temporary buffs + beautiful spectacles, but require high-RS clusters and combat readiness.

## 11. Corruption & Dissonance

### Sources of Corruption
1. **Dissonance Crystals:** Black crystalline nodes planted by Reset agents — emit anti-resonance that degrades nearby RS
2. **Mud Golem Residue:** Defeated golems leave "black sludge" that must be cleansed via fountain water
3. **Misaligned Buildings:** Structures below 50 RS generate passive dissonance in a small radius
4. **Overload Fallout:** Failed overloads can corrupt the triggering building
5. **Cassian's Betrayal (Moon 2):** Story event that introduces corruption mechanics

### Corruption Effects
- Visual: Golden veins turn black; buildings flicker with red-static
- Mechanical: -10% RS to all buildings within corruption radius per game-day
- NPC: Echoes become agitated, lose dialogue options, may flee the zone
- Extreme: Corrupted ley lines spawn "Sludge Golems" (elite enemies)

### Cleansing Methods
1. **Pure Water Fountain Spray:** Build/repair a fountain near corruption; ionized mist gradually cleanses
2. **9-Band Resonance Cannon:** Direct harmonic blast (combat ability) purges crystals
3. **Bell Tower Ring:** Scalar wave broadcast pushes corruption to zone edges
4. **Organ Performance:** Cathedral symphony in the corrupted zone (risk: dissonance interferes with tuning)

## 15. Progression & Endgame Power Scaling

### Early Game (Moons 1–4)
- Primarily 3-band structures
- Small domes, basic spires
- RS averaging 40–60
- Giant-mode bursts are rare and precious

### Mid Game (Moons 5–8)
- 6-band unlocked; healing and growth transform zones
- Star fort routing creates regional networks
- RS averaging 60–80
- Airships and trains become primary transport
- Cathedral symphonies begin producing spectacular effects

### Late Game (Moons 9–12)
- 9-band mastery; anti-grav, consciousness buffs, echo access
- Multi-continent ley-line network
- RS averaging 80–95
- Overloads become strategic tools
- Bell tower networks broadcast across the planet

### Endgame (Moon 13 + Post-Game)
- Global grid at 100%
- All buildings at 90+ RS
- Perfect Resonance clusters produce 243× multipliers
- True Timeline convergence accessible
- "Golden Age Mode" — infinite Aether, creative sandbox, no combat unless desired

## 16. Economy Balancing Targets

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Aether earned per 5-min session | 500–800 | Enough to restore 1 small building or half a large one |
| Aether cost: basic dome | 300–500 | Achievable in one good session |
| Aether cost: star fort | 2,000–3,000 | Multi-session goal (3–5 sessions) |
| Aether cost: cathedral | 5,000–8,000 | Major milestone (1–2 weeks of daily play) |
| Rewarded ad bonus | +50% session harvest | Meaningful but not required |
| Subscription bonus | Unlimited energy | Removes energy gate without power advantage |
| Giant mode affordability | 1–2 minutes per session | Frequent enough to feel powerful; scarce enough to feel special |
| Overload frequency | 1–2 per week of regular play | Spectacular but not routine |
| Full grid completion | ~3–4 months of daily play | Long enough for retention; achievable enough to not frustrate |

**Core Balance Principle:** A free player progresses at 60–70% the speed of a subscriber. The gap is in *convenience*, never in *power*. Both paths reach 100% grid and the True Timeline.
