## Moon Mechanics Overview

Each Moon follows a **28-day in-game cycle** with four 7-day phases (weeks):
- **Week 1 (Days 1–7):** Introduction — new mechanic tutorial, exploration, first encounters
- **Week 2 (Days 8–14):** Escalation — mechanic depth increases, companion development, mystery deepens
- **Week 3 (Days 15–21):** Crisis — combat intensifies, moral choices, major revelations
- **Week 4 (Days 22–28):** Crescendo — Moon-ending spectacle, boss encounter, cosmic event

**Universal Systems Active Every Moon:**
- Aether Harvesting (9-band spectrum, 432 Hz base tuning)
- Resonance Grid Progress (percentage tracked globally)
- 17-Hour Clock (alignment windows for bonus events)
- Companion Banter (context-sensitive, mood-reactive)
- Dissonance Threat Level (escalates Moon over Moon)

## Moon 1 — Magnetic Moon: Awakening

*Theme: Discovery, First Awe, Bloodline Activation*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Resonance Scanning**
- Tap-and-hold on buried structures to emit a sonar-like resonance pulse
- Pulse reveals structure outlines under mud layers — glow intensity indicates Aether concentration
- Deeper structures require longer holds (1s surface → 3s deep vault)
- HUD overlay: wireframe ghost of buried building appears, color-coded by restoration difficulty
  - Green: Minor debris clearing (5 minutes)
  - Yellow: Structural reconstruction needed (15 minutes)
  - Red: Deep Aether corruption — requires purification first (30+ minutes)

**Mud Excavation**
- Progressive dig mechanic: swipe gestures clear mud in layers
- Haptic feedback intensifies as you approach intact architecture
- Discovery moments: first glimpse of ornate stonework triggers slow-mo + choir sting
- Environmental storytelling: mud layers contain artifacts (newspaper fragments, broken tools, children's toys) that piece together the Flood narrative

**Dome Tuning**
- Restored domes require harmonic calibration via touch-wheel interface
- Match the dome's natural resonance frequency (mini-game: waveform alignment)
- Successfully tuned domes emit visible Aether columns — these connect to the global ley-line grid
- First dome tuning triggers a cinematic: golden light erupts upward, nearby bells ring sympathetically, fountains spontaneously activate

**Giant-Mode Burst**
- Triggered by giant skeleton "key" discovery (DLC 1 tie-in)
- 60-second transformation: player grows to giant scale, can move megaliths bare-handed
- Giant perspective reveals hidden inscriptions on building tops, invisible from ground level
- Cooldown: 1 full in-game day between uses

### Companion Layer

**Milo (Fox Spirit)**
- Introduced Day 1 — emerges from the first dome's ventilation shaft
- **Ability:** Highlights buried Aether veins (passive aura, 15m radius)
- **Banter triggers:** First excavation, first dome tuning, first giant-mode, nightfall
- **Personality:** Snarky, protective, deeply loyal — covers fear with humor
- **Growth:** Week 1 cautious → Week 4 actively leads player to discoveries

**Lirael (Spectral Architect)**
- Introduced Day 7 — manifests during first dome tuning
- **Ability:** Projects holographic blueprints of original structures over ruins
- **Banter triggers:** Architecture discovery, lore fragment, emotional story beat
- **Personality:** Melancholic wisdom, ancient perspective, gentle grief
- **Growth:** Initially fragmented/flickering → stabilizes as grid strengthens

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Mud Golems (animated Flood debris)
- Weak to resonance attacks (tuning fork weapon)
- Shatter into collectible Aether shards on defeat
- Larger golems require giant-mode or resonance traps

**Engagement Style:** Defensive — enemies emerge during restoration, player protects work-in-progress
- Golem waves trigger at 25%, 50%, 75% restoration checkpoints
- Completion reward: permanent zone purification (no further spawns)

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Dome Awakening**
- First fully restored cathedral dome performs its inaugural resonance broadcast
- Sequence: cathedral fills with golden light → dome crown ignites with Aether → beam shoots skyward → nearby buried structures partially surface, drawn upward by sympathetic resonance → aurora bands appear overhead
- Player watches from newly unlocked bell tower vantage point
- All companions present, contextual dialogue
- Unlocks: Moon 2 zone access, advanced scanning tools, Lirael's blueprint projection upgrade

## Moon 2 — Lunar Moon: Shadow & Purge

*Theme: Corruption, Internal Threat, First Real Loss*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Dissonance Detection**
- New tool: Dissonance Lens — toggleable overlay that reveals corruption veins in structures
- Corruption appears as dark fractal patterns spreading through stone and metal
- Detection range increases with Aether investment (upgradeable 10m → 25m → 50m)
- Corrupted zones cause haptic "wrongness" — irregular vibrations that feel off-rhythm

**Micro-Giant Mode**
- Inverse of giant mode: shrink to enter fractal interior architecture of domes
- Inside, domes are impossibly vast — cathedral-within-cathedral geometry
- Navigate crystal lattice corridors to reach corruption nodes
- Each node is a puzzle: match the original resonance pattern while dissonance tries to overwrite it
- Successful purges cause the dome to "breathe" — visible expansion and contraction

**Corruption Purge**
- Three-stage purge per corrupted zone:
  1. **Identify** — Use Dissonance Lens to map all corruption veins in the structure
  2. **Isolate** — Activate bell tower resonance to contain spread (rhythm mini-game)
  3. **Purify** — Enter micro-giant mode, destroy corruption crystals at their roots
- Failed purge: corruption spreads to an adjacent zone (real consequences, adds urgency)

**Bell Tower Purification**
- Bell towers serve as "immune system" for the grid
- Ringing a purified bell tower creates a resonance shield (visible golden dome) that blocks corruption spread
- Towers must be rung in sequence matching the ley-line geometry — wrong order weakens the shield
- Master purification: ringing 3 connected towers simultaneously creates a permanent ward

### Companion Layer

**Cassian (Introduced Day 3)**
- Appears as a charming scholar studying the corruption
- **Ability:** Analyzes corruption patterns, provides weak-point data
- **Hidden agenda:** Actually planting dissonance crystals (revealed Moon 7)
- **Banter:** Overly helpful, subtly steers player away from certain discoveries
- Experienced players may notice inconsistencies in his advice

**Lirael (Escalation)**
- Dissonance causes her projection to fracture — visual distortion when near corruption
- **New ability:** Can "remember" a structure's pre-corruption state, providing purge targets
- Emotional beats: corruption reminds her of the original Flood — dialogue becomes more urgent

**Milo (Development)**
- Growls near corruption sources (early warning system)
- **New ability:** Can dig through corrupted mud faster than player
- Becomes protective of Lirael — positions between her and corruption sources

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Fractal Wraiths (corruption-born entities)
- Phase through solid matter — cannot be blocked, only dodged or resonance-countered
- Drain Aether from nearby structures if not defeated quickly
- Elite variant: Mirror Wraiths — copy player's last three attacks

**Engagement Style:** Aggressive — enemies seek out and attack restoration sites
- Nighttime (in-game) increases spawn rate by 40%
- Bell tower resonance shields provide safe zones during waves

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Fractal Corruption Purge**
- Massive corruption bloom threatens the Moon 1 cathedral
- Player must coordinate bell towers, companions, and micro-giant purges simultaneously
- Climax: entering the cathedral's deepest fractal layer reveals a pre-Flood memory — the original architect's final moments as the dissonance struck
- Purge success: cathedral's organ spontaneously plays a chord that resonates across the entire restored grid
- Unlocks: Moon 3 zone, Micro-Giant Mode II (longer duration), corruption resistance upgrades

## Moon 3 — Electric Moon: Compassion & Rails

*Theme: Empathy, Found Family, Spectral Children, Resonance Rail Network*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Spectral Echo Adoption System**
- Discover orphan echoes at abandoned train stations and buried schoolhouses
- Each child is a unique spectral NPC with personality, backstory, and memories
- Adoption sequence: gentle approach (crouch + slow walk) → resonance lullaby (rhythm mini-game) → trust established → echo joins your settlement

**Named Orphan Echoes:**
- **Aria (age ~8):** Remembers singing stone-cutters. Quiet, observant. Hums melodies that reveal hidden frequencies. Once adopted, she occasionally points at structures and says "That one's humming wrong" — she's always right.
- **Toren (age ~11):** Remembers the train engineers. Bold, protective of younger echoes. Can "feel" where the rails want to go — invaluable during track restoration. Carries a toy wrench he refuses to let go of.
- **Syl (age ~6):** Remembers the fountains. Playful, easily frightened by dissonance. Her laughter causes nearby water to form geometric patterns. Won't speak about the day the fountains stopped.

**Resonance Rail Reactivation**
- Excavate buried rail segments using enhanced scanning from Moon 1
- Rails are not metal — they're crystallized Aether that must be re-tuned
- Track restoration mini-game: align rail segments by matching their resonance frequencies
- Successfully connected rail segments create visible golden energy paths between them
- First train reactivation: cinematic of crystalline train materializing on tuned tracks, spectral children pressing faces against windows

**Orphan Train Escort**
- Escort missions: guide spectral trains through hostile territory
- Train speed controlled by rail resonance quality — poorly tuned rails = slow, dangerous
- Children sing from the train cars — their 432 Hz lullabies heal zones the train passes through
- Zone healing is permanent — train routes become safe corridors

**Service Buff Multiplier**
- Each adopted child provides a passive buff based on their memory type:
  - Stone-cutter memories: +15% rock cutting precision
  - Engineer memories: +20% rail restoration speed
  - Fountain memories: +10% water purification range
- Buffs stack but cap at 5 children per settlement
- Children develop new abilities over time based on settlement happiness

### Expanded Companion Banter

**Lirael + Orphans:**
> "They're singing the old songs. The ones we sang when the world was whole.
> I taught some of those songs, you know. Before. I didn't think anyone remembered."

**Milo + Toren:**
> Toren: "Your fox growled at me."
> Milo: *ears flat, sheepish whine*
> Toren: "...okay, you can stay. But no growling during bedtime stories."

**Aria + Player (during rail restoration):**
> Aria: "Listen. The track is breathing. Can you hear it?"
> [Player tunes rail segment]
> Aria: "That's it. That's the song my father used to sing to the rails."

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Rail Wraiths (corrupted train guardians) + Dissonance Harvesters
- Rail Wraiths ride the corrupted sections — must be defeated before tracks can be tuned
- Harvesters try to drain the children's spectral energy — high priority targets
- Children's singing creates combat buffs: +10% damage in 432 Hz resonance zone

**Engagement Style:** Escort defense — protect children and trains simultaneously
- Mobile combat along rail lines — fighting while the train moves
- Environmental advantage: tuned rail segments damage enemies that cross them

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Orphan Train Escort** *(See also: [11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md](11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md))*
- Full scripted climax: escort the largest spectral train across three zones
- Children's combined singing creates a visible golden dome around the train
- Final segment: Dissonance Leviathan attacks — massive boss fight on and around a moving train
- Victory: children's lullaby purifies the leviathan, releasing a trapped giant echo
- Unlock: Moon 4 zone, Orphan Settlement expansion, Rail Network fast-travel system

## Moon 4 — Self-Existing Moon: Precision & Fortification

*Theme: Mastery, Sacred Geometry, Star Fort Architecture, Korath's First Echo*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Golden-Ratio Integrity System**
- All construction during Moon 4 must conform to φ (1.618…) proportions
- New HUD overlay: golden-ratio grid that shows optimal placement guides
- Structures placed on-ratio receive permanent Aether bonuses (+25% output)
- Off-ratio placement still works but structures degrade 3× faster
- Visual feedback: on-ratio placement causes brief golden shimmer and satisfying harmonic tone

**Precision Rock Cutting**
- Advanced construction mechanic: use resonance frequency to cut stone to exact dimensions
- Mini-game: hold touch to build resonance → release at peak harmonic (precision timing)
- Perfect cuts: stone glows briefly, edges are mathematically exact, fits seamlessly
- Good cuts: functional but visible seam lines, reduced Aether conductivity
- Failed cuts: stone shatters, resources partially lost, nearby structures wince (living architecture reacts)
- Mastery progression: simple blocks → complex curves → sacred geometry patterns → star fort bastions

**Star Fort Bastion Construction**
- Star forts are the most complex construction project in the game
- Multi-stage build: foundation (φ-ratio layout) → walls (precision cut stone) → bastions (angled for maximum resonance reflection) → moat (pure water resonance channel) → central tower (bell tower anchor)
- Each bastion point must align with a ley-line vector — placement puzzle using compass and grid overlay
- Completed bastions create resonance amplification zones that boost all nearby productivity
- Full star fort: becomes a **ley-line router**, connecting distant grid segments through its geometry

**Bell Tower Resonance Anchor**
- Star fort central tower serves as a permanent resonance anchor
- Anchor function: stabilizes all connected ley-line segments (prevents corruption decay)
- Bell tuning mini-game: match the tower's bell to the star fort's geometric frequency
- Successful tuning: tower emits a permanent low hum audible across the zone (ambient audio layer)
- Network effect: connected anchors create "harmony corridors" — safe travel routes between forts

### Korath's First Echo (Mid-Moon Event, ~Day 14)

**Discovery:** During deep excavation beneath the star fort foundation, player uncovers a stasis chamber
- Chamber contains residual energy pattern — not a body, but an echo of the giant Korath
- Echo speaks in fragments, disoriented, not fully aware

**Korath's Dialogue (Fragmented):**
> "The… geometry. It holds. Even now, it holds. Who…?"
> "You. Small one. You can hear the stone speak?"
> "We cut for millennia. Each block a prayer. Each wall a verse."
> "The Flood… the sound inverted. Our song became our cage."
> "Find the remaining echoes. We will build again. Together."

**Mechanical Impact:**
- Korath echo provides passive construction bonus: +30% stone integrity
- Can "preview" completed structures — shows hologram of finished star fort
- Advances the emotional arc toward his full appearance in Moon 7

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Siege Golems (massive, armored) + Precision Disruptors
- Siege Golems attack star fort walls — must be defeated before they breach
- Precision Disruptors emit anti-resonance that throws off your golden-ratio alignment
- Giant mode required for Siege Golem elites

**Engagement Style:** Tower defense hybrid — position resonance weaponry on bastions
- Bastion turrets: resonance cannons that fire concentrated harmonic pulses
- Moat defense: pure water channels slow enemies and damage corrupted targets
- Bell tower activation: area-wide stun on all enemies in range (30-second cooldown)

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Star Fort Siege** *(See also: [11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md](11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md))*
- Full scripted climax: coordinated assault on your newly built star fort
- Four-phase battle: Breach → Coordinated Defense → Bell Tower Stand → Resolution
- Korath's echo provides tactical guidance via dialogue during combat
- Victory: star fort fully activates as planetary router, visible from space (camera zooms to orbital view)
- Unlock: Moon 5 zone, advanced construction tools, Korath echo joins as permanent advisor

## Moons 5–13 — Structured Outlines

### Moon 5 — Overtone Moon: Amplification & Harmony

*Theme: Grid expansion reaches critical mass*
- **Core Mechanic:** Overtone Resonance — previously tuned structures begin harmonizing with each other automatically, creating feedback loops
- **New System:** Grid Amplification Networks — connect 5+ structures to create "resonance circuits" that produce new Aether frequencies
- **Companion:** Thorne deepens involvement — reveals pre-Flood airship culture
- **Combat:** Resonance Overload enemies — drawn to high-concentration grid nodes
- **Moon-End:** First intercontinental ley-line connection lights up (visible aurora bridge between zones)

### Moon 6 — Rhythmic Moon: The Pipe Organ Requiem

*Theme: Music as weapon, construction tool, and cosmic language*
- **Core Mechanic:** Full Pipe Organ Conduction (see [13_MINI_GAMES.md](13_MINI_GAMES.md))
- **New System:** Ionized mist rain from organ performances — different compositions create different environmental effects
- **Companion:** Cathedral organist echo (new companion), Lirael's memories of music return
- **Combat:** Sound-based enemies that can only be defeated through specific harmonic patterns
- **Moon-End:** Cymatic Requiem performance — city-wide event, mist rain grows giant-scale resources

### Moon 7 — Resonant Moon: Giant's Awakening

*Theme: Korath fully manifests, ancient wisdom returns*
- **Core Mechanic:** Full Giant Mode — permanent giant-scale abilities (not time-limited)
- **New System:** Giant-scale construction — build at 10× scale with Korath's guidance
- **Companion:** Korath joins as full companion (largest party member, unique dialogue)
- **Combat:** Titan-class enemies — only defeatable in giant mode
- **Moon-End:** Korath's teaching complete — player can now cut stone at any scale with perfect precision

### Moon 8 — Galactic Moon: The Airship Armada

*Theme: The sky remembers, aerial civilization returns*
- **Core Mechanic:** Armada Fleet Management — coordinate multiple airships, assign routes
- **New System:** Aerial construction — build floating platforms and sky docks
- **Companion:** Thorne commands lead airship, formations create resonance patterns
- **Combat:** Aerial raids on Reset supply lines, dogfighting with corrupted craft
- **Moon-End:** Full armada flyover of restored zones — continental celebration

### Moon 9 — Solar Moon: The Ley Line Prophecy

*Theme: Prophecy stones reveal the full truth*
- **Core Mechanic:** Prophecy Stone Collection (12 stones across all zones)
- **New System:** Temporal glimpses — each stone shows a preserved pre-Flood moment
- **Companion:** All companions react to their personal connections in the prophecy
- **Combat:** Prophecy Guardians — test worthiness before surrendering stones
- **Moon-End:** Alignment of 12 stones creates temporary floating aurora city

### Moon 10 — Planetary Moon: The Living Grid

*Theme: The grid becomes sentient, ley lines pulse with purpose*
- **Core Mechanic:** Grid Consciousness — the resonance network develops awareness, communicates
- **New System:** Grid Requests — the network asks for specific restorations, rewards compliance
- **Companion:** Korath recognizes the grid's voice — "This is what we always intended"
- **Combat:** Dissonant One's forces make desperate assault on key grid nodes
- **Moon-End:** Grid achieves 90% — planet begins to visibly heal (forests grow, waters clear)

### Moon 11 — Spectral Moon: Veil Between Worlds

*Theme: Boundaries thin, echoes become more real*
- **Core Mechanic:** Spectral Overlap — pre-Flood world becomes partially visible/interactable
- **New System:** Echo Architecture — build with materials from both timelines simultaneously
- **Companion:** All spectral companions (Lirael, echoes, orphans) begin to solidify
- **Combat:** Cross-timeline enemies — attack from the echo realm
- **Moon-End:** First stable portal between current world and Golden Age echo opens

### Moon 12 — Crystal Moon: Planetary Bell-Tower Ring

*Theme: Unified sound, global resonance event*
- **Core Mechanic:** Bell Tower Synchronization across ALL restored towers (see [13_MINI_GAMES.md](13_MINI_GAMES.md))
- **New System:** Planetary Resonance Score — all towers contribute to a single harmonic chord
- **Companion:** Every companion takes position at a different tower
- **Combat:** Final Dissonant One counterstrike — corruption surges across all zones simultaneously
- **Moon-End:** Planetary Bell-Tower Ring — all towers ring in unison, visible shockwave circles globe

### Moon 13 — Cosmic Moon: True Timeline Convergence

*Theme: Destiny, choice, cosmic harmony restored*
- **Core Mechanic:** Timeline manipulation — all three ending paths available
- **New System:** Reality editing — player can reshape zones based on timeline choice
- **Companion:** All companions deliver final personal arcs
- **Combat:** Dissonant One final confrontation — cosmic-scale battle
- **Moon-End:** True Timeline Convergence event — sky cracks, Golden Age returns (or doesn't)
