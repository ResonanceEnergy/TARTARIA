## Moon Mechanics Overview

Each Moon follows a **28-day in-game cycle** with four 7-day phases (weeks):
- **Week 1 (Days 1–7):** Introduction — new mechanic tutorial, exploration, first encounters
- **Week 2 (Days 8–14):** Escalation — mechanic depth increases, companion development, mystery deepens
- **Week 3 (Days 15–21):** Crisis — combat intensifies, moral choices, major revelations
- **Week 4 (Days 22–28):** Crescendo — Moon-ending spectacle, boss encounter, cosmic event

**Universal Systems Active Every Moon:**
- Aether Harvesting (9-band spectrum, 432 Hz base tuning)
- Resonance Grid Progress (percentage tracked globally)
- 17-Hour Clock (alignment windows for bonus events)
- Companion Banter (context-sensitive, mood-reactive)
- Dissonance Threat Level (escalates Moon over Moon)

## Moon 1 — Magnetic Moon: Awakening

*Theme: Discovery, First Awe, Bloodline Activation*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Resonance Scanning**
- Tap-and-hold on buried structures to emit a sonar-like resonance pulse
- Pulse reveals structure outlines under mud layers — glow intensity indicates Aether concentration
- Deeper structures require longer holds (1s surface → 3s deep vault)
- HUD overlay: wireframe ghost of buried building appears, color-coded by restoration difficulty
  - Green: Minor debris clearing (5 minutes)
  - Yellow: Structural reconstruction needed (15 minutes)
  - Red: Deep Aether corruption — requires purification first (30+ minutes)

**Mud Excavation**
- Progressive dig mechanic: swipe gestures clear mud in layers
- Haptic feedback intensifies as you approach intact architecture
- Discovery moments: first glimpse of ornate stonework triggers slow-mo + choir sting
- Environmental storytelling: mud layers contain artifacts (newspaper fragments, broken tools, children's toys) that piece together the Flood narrative

**Dome Tuning**
- Restored domes require harmonic calibration via touch-wheel interface
- Match the dome's natural resonance frequency (mini-game: waveform alignment)
- Successfully tuned domes emit visible Aether columns — these connect to the global ley-line grid
- First dome tuning triggers a cinematic: golden light erupts upward, nearby bells ring sympathetically, fountains spontaneously activate

**Giant-Mode Burst**
- Triggered by giant skeleton "key" discovery (DLC 1 tie-in)
- 60-second transformation: player grows to giant scale, can move megaliths bare-handed
- Giant perspective reveals hidden inscriptions on building tops, invisible from ground level
- Cooldown: 1 full in-game day between uses

### Companion Layer

**Milo (Fox Spirit)**
- Introduced Day 1 — emerges from the first dome's ventilation shaft
- **Ability:** Highlights buried Aether veins (passive aura, 15m radius)
- **Banter triggers:** First excavation, first dome tuning, first giant-mode, nightfall
- **Personality:** Snarky, protective, deeply loyal — covers fear with humor
- **Growth:** Week 1 cautious → Week 4 actively leads player to discoveries

**Lirael (Spectral Architect)**
- Introduced Day 7 — manifests during first dome tuning
- **Ability:** Projects holographic blueprints of original structures over ruins
- **Banter triggers:** Architecture discovery, lore fragment, emotional story beat
- **Personality:** Melancholic wisdom, ancient perspective, gentle grief
- **Growth:** Initially fragmented/flickering → stabilizes as grid strengthens

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Mud Golems (animated Flood debris)
- Weak to resonance attacks (tuning fork weapon)
- Shatter into collectible Aether shards on defeat
- Larger golems require giant-mode or resonance traps

**Engagement Style:** Defensive — enemies emerge during restoration, player protects work-in-progress
- Golem waves trigger at 25%, 50%, 75% restoration checkpoints
- Completion reward: permanent zone purification (no further spawns)

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Dome Awakening**
- First fully restored cathedral dome performs its inaugural resonance broadcast
- Sequence: cathedral fills with golden light → dome crown ignites with Aether → beam shoots skyward → nearby buried structures partially surface, drawn upward by sympathetic resonance → aurora bands appear overhead
- Player watches from newly unlocked bell tower vantage point
- All companions present, contextual dialogue
- Unlocks: Moon 2 zone access, advanced scanning tools, Lirael's blueprint projection upgrade

## Moon 2 — Lunar Moon: Shadow & Purge

*Theme: Corruption, Internal Threat, First Real Loss*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Dissonance Detection**
- New tool: Dissonance Lens — toggleable overlay that reveals corruption veins in structures
- Corruption appears as dark fractal patterns spreading through stone and metal
- Detection range increases with Aether investment (upgradeable 10m → 25m → 50m)
- Corrupted zones cause haptic "wrongness" — irregular vibrations that feel off-rhythm

**Micro-Giant Mode**
- Inverse of giant mode: shrink to enter fractal interior architecture of domes
- Inside, domes are impossibly vast — cathedral-within-cathedral geometry
- Navigate crystal lattice corridors to reach corruption nodes
- Each node is a puzzle: match the original resonance pattern while dissonance tries to overwrite it
- Successful purges cause the dome to "breathe" — visible expansion and contraction

**Corruption Purge**
- Three-stage purge per corrupted zone:
  1. **Identify** — Use Dissonance Lens to map all corruption veins in the structure
  2. **Isolate** — Activate bell tower resonance to contain spread (rhythm mini-game)
  3. **Purify** — Enter micro-giant mode, destroy corruption crystals at their roots
- Failed purge: corruption spreads to an adjacent zone (real consequences, adds urgency)

**Bell Tower Purification**
- Bell towers serve as "immune system" for the grid
- Ringing a purified bell tower creates a resonance shield (visible golden dome) that blocks corruption spread
- Towers must be rung in sequence matching the ley-line geometry — wrong order weakens the shield
- Master purification: ringing 3 connected towers simultaneously creates a permanent ward

### Companion Layer

**Cassian (Introduced Day 3)**
- Appears as a charming scholar studying the corruption
- **Ability:** Analyzes corruption patterns, provides weak-point data
- **Hidden agenda:** Actually planting dissonance crystals (revealed Moon 7)
- **Banter:** Overly helpful, subtly steers player away from certain discoveries
- Experienced players may notice inconsistencies in his advice

**Lirael (Escalation)**
- Dissonance causes her projection to fracture — visual distortion when near corruption
- **New ability:** Can "remember" a structure's pre-corruption state, providing purge targets
- Emotional beats: corruption reminds her of the original Flood — dialogue becomes more urgent

**Milo (Development)**
- Growls near corruption sources (early warning system)
- **New ability:** Can dig through corrupted mud faster than player
- Becomes protective of Lirael — positions between her and corruption sources

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Fractal Wraiths (corruption-born entities)
- Phase through solid matter — cannot be blocked, only dodged or resonance-countered
- Drain Aether from nearby structures if not defeated quickly
- Elite variant: Mirror Wraiths — copy player's last three attacks

**Engagement Style:** Aggressive — enemies seek out and attack restoration sites
- Nighttime (in-game) increases spawn rate by 40%
- Bell tower resonance shields provide safe zones during waves

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Fractal Corruption Purge**
- Massive corruption bloom threatens the Moon 1 cathedral
- Player must coordinate bell towers, companions, and micro-giant purges simultaneously
- Climax: entering the cathedral's deepest fractal layer reveals a pre-Flood memory — the original architect's final moments as the dissonance struck
- Purge success: cathedral's organ spontaneously plays a chord that resonates across the entire restored grid
- Unlocks: Moon 3 zone, Micro-Giant Mode II (longer duration), corruption resistance upgrades

## Moon 3 — Electric Moon: Compassion & Rails

*Theme: Empathy, Found Family, Spectral Children, Resonance Rail Network*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Spectral Echo Adoption System**
- Discover orphan echoes at abandoned train stations and buried schoolhouses
- Each child is a unique spectral NPC with personality, backstory, and memories
- Adoption sequence: gentle approach (crouch + slow walk) → resonance lullaby (rhythm mini-game) → trust established → echo joins your settlement

**Named Orphan Echoes:**
- **Aria (age ~8):** Remembers singing stone-cutters. Quiet, observant. Hums melodies that reveal hidden frequencies. Once adopted, she occasionally points at structures and says "That one's humming wrong" — she's always right.
- **Toren (age ~11):** Remembers the train engineers. Bold, protective of younger echoes. Can "feel" where the rails want to go — invaluable during track restoration. Carries a toy wrench he refuses to let go of.
- **Syl (age ~6):** Remembers the fountains. Playful, easily frightened by dissonance. Her laughter causes nearby water to form geometric patterns. Won't speak about the day the fountains stopped.

**Resonance Rail Reactivation**
- Excavate buried rail segments using enhanced scanning from Moon 1
- Rails are not metal — they're crystallized Aether that must be re-tuned
- Track restoration mini-game: align rail segments by matching their resonance frequencies
- Successfully connected rail segments create visible golden energy paths between them
- First train reactivation: cinematic of crystalline train materializing on tuned tracks, spectral children pressing faces against windows

**Orphan Train Escort**
- Escort missions: guide spectral trains through hostile territory
- Train speed controlled by rail resonance quality — poorly tuned rails = slow, dangerous
- Children sing from the train cars — their 432 Hz lullabies heal zones the train passes through
- Zone healing is permanent — train routes become safe corridors

**Service Buff Multiplier**
- Each adopted child provides a passive buff based on their memory type:
  - Stone-cutter memories: +15% rock cutting precision
  - Engineer memories: +20% rail restoration speed
  - Fountain memories: +10% water purification range
- Buffs stack but cap at 5 children per settlement
- Children develop new abilities over time based on settlement happiness

### Expanded Companion Banter

**Lirael + Orphans:**
> "They're singing the old songs. The ones we sang when the world was whole.
> I taught some of those songs, you know. Before. I didn't think anyone remembered."

**Milo + Toren:**
> Toren: "Your fox growled at me."
> Milo: *ears flat, sheepish whine*
> Toren: "...okay, you can stay. But no growling during bedtime stories."

**Aria + Player (during rail restoration):**
> Aria: "Listen. The track is breathing. Can you hear it?"
> [Player tunes rail segment]
> Aria: "That's it. That's the song my father used to sing to the rails."

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Rail Wraiths (corrupted train guardians) + Dissonance Harvesters
- Rail Wraiths ride the corrupted sections — must be defeated before tracks can be tuned
- Harvesters try to drain the children's spectral energy — high priority targets
- Children's singing creates combat buffs: +10% damage in 432 Hz resonance zone

**Engagement Style:** Escort defense — protect children and trains simultaneously
- Mobile combat along rail lines — fighting while the train moves
- Environmental advantage: tuned rail segments damage enemies that cross them

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Orphan Train Escort** *(See also: [11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md](11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md))*
- Full scripted climax: escort the largest spectral train across three zones
- Children's combined singing creates a visible golden dome around the train
- Final segment: Dissonance Leviathan attacks — massive boss fight on and around a moving train
- Victory: children's lullaby purifies the leviathan, releasing a trapped giant echo
- Unlock: Moon 4 zone, Orphan Settlement expansion, Rail Network fast-travel system

## Moon 4 — Self-Existing Moon: Precision & Fortification

*Theme: Mastery, Sacred Geometry, Star Fort Architecture, Korath's First Echo*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Golden-Ratio Integrity System**
- All construction during Moon 4 must conform to φ (1.618…) proportions
- New HUD overlay: golden-ratio grid that shows optimal placement guides
- Structures placed on-ratio receive permanent Aether bonuses (+25% output)
- Off-ratio placement still works but structures degrade 3× faster
- Visual feedback: on-ratio placement causes brief golden shimmer and satisfying harmonic tone

**Precision Rock Cutting**
- Advanced construction mechanic: use resonance frequency to cut stone to exact dimensions
- Mini-game: hold touch to build resonance → release at peak harmonic (precision timing)
- Perfect cuts: stone glows briefly, edges are mathematically exact, fits seamlessly
- Good cuts: functional but visible seam lines, reduced Aether conductivity
- Failed cuts: stone shatters, resources partially lost, nearby structures wince (living architecture reacts)
- Mastery progression: simple blocks → complex curves → sacred geometry patterns → star fort bastions

**Star Fort Bastion Construction**
- Star forts are the most complex construction project in the game
- Multi-stage build: foundation (φ-ratio layout) → walls (precision cut stone) → bastions (angled for maximum resonance reflection) → moat (pure water resonance channel) → central tower (bell tower anchor)
- Each bastion point must align with a ley-line vector — placement puzzle using compass and grid overlay
- Completed bastions create resonance amplification zones that boost all nearby productivity
- Full star fort: becomes a **ley-line router**, connecting distant grid segments through its geometry

**Bell Tower Resonance Anchor**
- Star fort central tower serves as a permanent resonance anchor
- Anchor function: stabilizes all connected ley-line segments (prevents corruption decay)
- Bell tuning mini-game: match the tower's bell to the star fort's geometric frequency
- Successful tuning: tower emits a permanent low hum audible across the zone (ambient audio layer)
- Network effect: connected anchors create "harmony corridors" — safe travel routes between forts

### Korath's First Echo (Mid-Moon Event, ~Day 14)

**Discovery:** During deep excavation beneath the star fort foundation, player uncovers a stasis chamber
- Chamber contains residual energy pattern — not a body, but an echo of the giant Korath
- Echo speaks in fragments, disoriented, not fully aware

**Korath's Dialogue (Fragmented):**
> "The… geometry. It holds. Even now, it holds. Who…?"
> "You. Small one. You can hear the stone speak?"
> "We cut for millennia. Each block a prayer. Each wall a verse."
> "The Flood… the sound inverted. Our song became our cage."
> "Find the remaining echoes. We will build again. Together."

**Mechanical Impact:**
- Korath echo provides passive construction bonus: +30% stone integrity
- Can "preview" completed structures — shows hologram of finished star fort
- Advances the emotional arc toward his full appearance in Moon 7

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Siege Golems (massive, armored) + Precision Disruptors
- Siege Golems attack star fort walls — must be defeated before they breach
- Precision Disruptors emit anti-resonance that throws off your golden-ratio alignment
- Giant mode required for Siege Golem elites

**Engagement Style:** Tower defense hybrid — position resonance weaponry on bastions
- Bastion turrets: resonance cannons that fire concentrated harmonic pulses
- Moat defense: pure water channels slow enemies and damage corrupted targets
- Bell tower activation: area-wide stun on all enemies in range (30-second cooldown)

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Star Fort Siege** *(See also: [11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md](11_SCRIPTED_CLIMAXES.md))*
- Full scripted climax: coordinated assault on your newly built star fort
- Four-phase battle: Breach → Coordinated Defense → Bell Tower Stand → Resolution
- Korath's echo provides tactical guidance via dialogue during combat
- Victory: star fort fully activates as planetary router, visible from space (camera zooms to orbital view)
- Unlock: Moon 5 zone, advanced construction tools, Korath echo joins as permanent advisor

## Moon 5 — Overtone Moon: Amplification & Harmony

*Theme: Grid Expansion Reaches Critical Mass, White City Splendor*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Overtone Resonance System**
- Previously tuned structures begin harmonizing *automatically* — visible golden threads pulse between connected domes
- Overtone feedback loops: when 3+ structures reach 80% RS simultaneously, they generate a 4th harmonic frequency not present in any individual building
- Player receives "Overtone Alerts" — haptic pulse + map ping when a new feedback loop forms
- Overtone energy is harvestable: tap the golden threads to collect amplified Aether (2× yield vs. static harvest)
- Chain reactions: a sufficiently strong overtone loop can spontaneously tune a nearby un-restored structure to 25% RS, revealing its outline through the mud

**Grid Amplification Networks**
- New construction overlay: connect 5+ structures with precision-laid Aether conduit (golden-ratio spacing required)
- Conduit placement mini-game: trace a golden-spiral path between structure anchor points — closer to the ideal spiral = higher amplification
- Successfully formed networks produce new Aether frequencies unavailable from solo structures
  - 5-node ring: generates 6-Band healing aura (new capability)
  - 7-node star: generates 6-Band growth accelerator (crops, gardens, trees mature 3× faster)
  - 12-node dodecahedron: generates 6-Band communication channel (NPCs in network share information instantly)
- Network visualization: glowing geometric shapes float above connected zones at night — pentagons, hexagrams, dodecahedra made of pure light

**White City Pavilion Restoration**
- Five pavilions of extraordinary Beaux-Arts grandeur — fluted Corinthian columns, massive domes, ornate entablatures half-buried in silted mud
- Each pavilion specializes in a different Aether function:
  1. **Hall of Industry:** Resonance workshop — unlocks advanced crafting recipes
  2. **Palace of Mechanics:** Transit hub — future airship dock foundation
  3. **Hall of Agriculture:** Cymatic garden prototype — ionized-mist growing chambers
  4. **Pavilion of Electricity:** 6-Band amplifier — first structure to radiate healing auras
  5. **Central Grand Basin:** 20-foot fountain columns spray ionized mist into miniature auroras
- Pavilion excavation requires enhanced scanning from Moon 1 + golden-ratio templates from Moon 4
- Restored pavilions replay holographic pre-Flood festivals: giants and humans celebrating together, airships overhead, children on floating crystal platforms

**First Airship Dock Construction**
- Blueprints found in Palace of Mechanics basement — giant-scale technical drawings on metallic paper
- Dock construction: precision rock cutting (Moon 4 skill) for the platform, golden-ratio alignment for the landing array, pure water moat for anti-grav stabilization
- Dock emits a 6-Band homing signal once activated — this is what draws Thorne's ship in Moon 8

### Companion Layer

**Captain Thorne (Radio Voice — Introduction)**
- Crackling signal on the airship communicator found in Pavilion 2
- Grumpy, impatient, two centuries of solitary flight have not improved his temperament
- **Ability (Remote):** Provides aerial reconnaissance data — marks buried structures on map within radio range
- **Banter triggers:** Each pavilion restoration, dock completion, nightfall transmissions

> Thorne: "About time someone lit a signal. I've been circling for two centuries. Come to the White City — and bring something to drink."

> Thorne (after Pavilion 5): "I can see your fountains from 10,000 feet. Impressive. Now build me a dock before I run out of patience and mercury."

**Milo (White City Reaction)**
- Overwhelmed by the scale and beauty — drops his cynical merchant persona for the first time
- **New ability:** Appraises artifacts at 2× accuracy (inspired by genuine wonder)
- **Growth:** First moment of sincere emotion — "They bulldozed this?! For a parking lot and a hot dog stand?!"
- Begins voluntarily helping with restoration instead of hawking goods

**Lirael (Network Sensitivity)**
- Overtone networks cause her projection to stabilize — she can remain visible for hours instead of minutes
- **New ability:** Senses network health — warns of weak links before they break
- Connection to the music builds — she hums along with the overtone harmonics unconsciously

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Resonance Overload Entities (attracted to high-concentration grid nodes)
- Formed from excess Aether that crystallizes wrong — beautiful but hostile geometric shapes
- Shatter into prismatic fragments on defeat (collectible crafting materials)
- Elite variant: Overload Titans — require network-coordinated attacks (activate 3 amplification nodes simultaneously to expose weak point)

**Engagement Style:** Node defense — enemies swarm toward the highest-RS structures
- Grid Amplification Networks double as defensive perimeters — enemies that cross conduit lines take continuous resonance damage
- Network coordination: player must balance power output (higher RS = more production but attracts stronger enemies)
- First strategic layer: choose between max amplification (risky) and sustainable resonance (safe but slower)

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The First Intercontinental Connection**
- Final pavilion restoration completes the White City's central spire — the fragment from Moon 1 slots into place
- Spire ignites: golden beam shoots upward, splits into multiple threads arcing toward the horizon
- Camera follows the brightest thread across an ocean — it lands on a distant continent, illuminating a previously invisible star fort

## Moon 6 — Rhythmic Moon: The Pipe Organ Requiem

*Theme: Music as Weapon, Construction Tool, and Cosmic Language*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Crystal Pipe Reconstruction**
- The sunken cathedral's pipe organ has 32-foot bass pipes — some shattered, all detuned
- Excavate fractured crystal pipe segments from surrounding mud chambers
- Pipe repair: precision rock cutting (Moon 4) applied to crystalline material — resonance frequency must match the pipe's original harmonic signature
- Each repaired pipe unlocks a new note in the Conduction mini-game (see [13_MINI_GAMES.md](13_MINI_GAMES.md))
- Total pipes to restore: 61 (5 registers × 12 notes + 1 master resonance pipe)
- Cosmetic progression: repaired pipes glow from within — the organ gradually transforms from dark ruin to luminous instrument

**Hydraulic Bellows Restoration**
- The organ is powered by pressurized pure water — a hydraulic system connecting to the Moon 11 aquifer (foreshadowing)
- Restore fountain network feeding the bellows: excavate 3 fountain sources, lay precision-cut stone channels, connect to the organ's intake manifold
- Each fountain connection increases organ power: 1 fountain = solo register, 2 = duet, 3 = full symphony
- Water pressure tuning: too low = weak tones, too high = pipe shattering — Goldilocks mini-game with haptic feedback indicating pressure level

**Ionized Mist Rain System**
- Full organ performances generate ionized mist that rises through the cathedral's broken roof
- Different compositions create different environmental effects:
  - 3-6-9 ascending scale: healing rain — restores 15% RS to all structures in zone
  - Golden-ratio harmonic: growth rain — cymatic gardens produce giant-scale resources
  - Lirael's lullaby arrangement: calming rain — suppresses all dissonance in range for 2 hours
  - Discordant improvisation (mistake or deliberate): storm rain — damages structures, spawns enemies (risk/reward for advanced players)
- Rain effects are visible from adjacent zones — shimmer curtains descending from the sky

**Rose Window Cymatic Projection**
- Fully tuned organ causes rose windows to project kaleidoscopic cymatic mandalas onto cathedral floor and walls
- Mandala patterns encode hidden messages — lore fragments legible only during specific harmonic combinations
- One pattern reveals the organist's journal: the last piece played before the Flood was an unfinished composition titled "The Eternal Resonance"

### Companion Layer

**Veritas (Cathedral Organist Echo — New Companion)**
- Manifests at the organ bench on Day 3 — fingers hovering over keys, trapped mid-performance for centuries
- **Personality:** Precise, perfectionist, passionate — speaks in musical metaphors
- **Ability:** Teaches advanced organ techniques — player can learn multi-register compositions (mandatory for Requiem climax)
- **Growth:** Initially only able to play fragments → as pipes are restored, can play full pieces → joins the Requiem performance at climax

> Veritas: "The pipes are crying. Each note comes out backwards. Heal the crystal, child. Let the music remember its own name."

> Veritas (after full restoration): "I have waited four hundred years to finish this piece. Will you conduct while I play?"

**Lirael (Memory of Music Returns)**
- The organ's vibrations trigger deep memories — she remembers being a singer in the Golden Age
- **New ability:** Can conduct children's choirs — spectral orphans from Moon 3 arrive and harmonize
- Emotional climax: during the Requiem, she steps to a crystal microphone (ancient Tartarian PA) and sings a solo line from her lullaby — the moment she fully remembers who she was
- **Banter with Veritas:**

> Lirael: "I remember this cathedral. I sang here. Before."
> Veritas: "Your voice was the second register. We called you the Silver Passage."
> Lirael: "...I had a name before Lirael, didn't I?"
> Veritas: "You had a name that meant 'the note between silence and song.' I will play it for you when the organ is whole."

**Milo (Uncomfortable With Beauty)**
- Genuinely frightened by the organ's power — hides behind pews during performances
- Comedy relief that masks growing emotional investment
- **New ability:** Scouts discover organ component locations faster (motivated by wanting the "haunted concert hall" fixed ASAP)

> Milo (hiding behind a pew): "I signed up for treasure hunting, not a haunted concert hall!"
> Milo (after first full performance): "...okay. That was. Um. That was really something. Don't tell anyone I said that."

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Harmonic Parasites (sound-based entities)
- Feed on musical energy — spawn from the organ when wrong notes are played
- Can only be defeated by specific harmonic counter-patterns (B-flat triad defeats a D-minor parasite, etc.)
- Elite variant: Dissonant Conductor — coordinates multiple parasites into attack formations, must be "out-conducted" by the player performing a superior composition in real-time

**Engagement Style:** Performance combat — fighting IS playing
- Combat encounters occur during organ performances — enemies attack and the player must simultaneously manage note accuracy and defensive resonance blasts
- Bell towers (if previously purified) provide automatic support fire during organ battles
- Veritas covers one register while player handles the rest — team musical combat

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Cymatic Requiem** *(See also: [12_VIVID_VISUALS.md](12_VIVID_VISUALS.md))*
- Full 5-register organ symphony: player conducts while Veritas plays bass and pedal
- Lirael sings the solo Silver Passage — her spectral form glows incandescent
- Children's choir from Moon 3 joins from the nave — their 432 Hz lullaby weaves through the organ's thunder
- Performance accuracy tracked: Bronze (60%), Silver (80%), Gold (90%), Transcendent (98%+)
- At Gold+: cathedral achieves 100% RS and the Aether *overloads beautifully*
  - Rose windows blaze with kaleidoscopic light projected onto clouds above
  - City-wide ionized mist rain falls across two zones
  - Giant-scale resources bloom overnight — crops swell 10× in cymatic gardens
  - Every fountain in range sprays in synchronized rhythmic pulses
- **Lore Revelation:** The organ's tuning records show it was last calibrated by "Z." — Zereth. His calibration was *flawless*. If he was the villain, why does his work ring with such perfect harmony?
- Unlocks: Moon 7 zone, Lirael choir-conducting in all zones (passive buff), organ mechanics prerequisite for Moon 12's planetary bell sync, giant-scale resources fuel Moon 7's massive construction

## Moon 7 — Resonant Moon: Giant's Awakening

*Theme: Ancient Wisdom Returns, Betrayal & Choice, 9-Band Transcendence*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Giant Stasis Thawing**
- Multi-session excavation of the deepest mud vault yet — Aether ice encases a living giant
- Ice glows violet-aurora: 9-Band energy, stronger than anything previously encountered

## Moon 12 — Crystal Moon: Planetary Bell-Tower Ring

*Theme: Unified Sound, Global Cooperation, The Penultimate Hour*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Bell Tower Network Synchronization**
- 12 towers across 12 continents — each restored in previous Moons, each waiting for the signal to ring in unison
- Synchronization requires visiting every tower via the airship/train network and fine-tuning each bell to match its neighbors
- Each tower has a unique natural frequency determined by its construction materials, architecture, and ley-line position
- Tuning interface: the Bell Tower Synchronization mini-game from [13_MINI_GAMES.md](13_MINI_GAMES.md) at planetary complexity
  - Single tower: match the timing ring (rehearsed in earlier Moons)
  - Adjacent tower pair: match TWO simultaneous timing rings (new challenge)
  - Continental cluster: match FOUR rings with staggered timing (demanding)
  - Planetary chord: all 12 towers feeding into one interface — the ultimate test of every skill learned

**Planetary Resonance Score**
- New HUD element: a real-time score showing the combined harmonic output of all connected towers
- Score components:
  - **Frequency Accuracy:** How close each tower is to its optimal pitch (±1 Hz tolerance)
  - **Phase Alignment:** Whether towers ring in sequence at the correct intervals
  - **Harmonic Complexity:** The richness of the combined chord (more unique frequencies = higher score)
  - **Sustain Duration:** How long the combined resonance maintains after the initial ring
- Target score for the Planetary Ring event: 95/100 minimum — anything less and the cascade fails to reach global coverage
- Real-time feedback: as you tune each tower, the score incrementally rises — visible progress toward the cosmic goal

**Integration of All Previous Mechanics**
- Moon 12 deliberately tests mastery of every system introduced throughout the game:
  - **Excavation (Moon 1):** Some towers are still partially buried — quick-dig to access tunables
  - **Purification (Moon 2):** Dissonance crystals appear at 4 towers during the final assault — must be purged before tuning
  - **Rail Access (Moon 3):** Continental rail network provides the fastest travel between towers
  - **Golden-Ratio (Moon 4):** Tower structural integrity checks require golden-ratio analysis
  - **Grid Amplification (Moon 5):** Network connections between towers must be maintained during tuning
  - **Organ Conduction (Moon 6):** Three towers have organ components that require musical skill to activate
  - **Giant Mode (Moon 7):** Two towers have giant-scale bells that can only be struck in giant form
  - **Airship (Moon 8):** Aerial access required for 3 tower locations on floating platforms
  - **Prophecy (Moon 9):** The 12th Prophecy Stone is embedded in the final tower's bell
  - **Train (Moon 10):** Continental travel for efficient tower-hopping
  - **Fountain (Moon 11):** Ionized mist required to cool overheating bells during sustained ringing

### Companion Layer

**Every Companion at a Different Tower**
- Each companion takes position at the tower that resonates most with their story:
  - **Milo:** Tower 1 (New Chicago) — where it all began
  - **Lirael:** Tower 6 (Sunken Cathedral) — where she remembered music
  - **Veritas:** Tower 6 (assists Lirael with organ-tower hybrid)
  - **Thorne:** Tower 8 (Floating Sky Isle) — his element
  - **Korath's Echo:** Tower 7 (Star Fort Cluster) — where he sacrificed
  - **Toren:** Tower 10 (Continental Rail Hub) — his engineering pride
  - **Aria:** Tower 3 (Rail Junction) — she hears frequencies others miss
  - **Syl:** Tower 11 (Aquifer Sanctum) — where the water sings
  - **Cassian (if redeemed):** Tower 2 — atoning where he first betrayed
  - **Cassian (if purged):** Tower 2 — his ghost howls but cannot prevent the tuning
- Companion communication: radio/resonance links allow real-time dialogue during the synchronization sequence
- Each companion's bell-strike timing is based on their personality — Thorne is military precise, Milo is slightly early (eager), Lirael waits for the exact emotional moment

> Milo (at Tower 1): "So let me get this straight — we're tuning the planet like a guitar? And if we hit a wrong note…?"
> Thorne (over radio): "Then we'll feel it. Everyone will."
> Korath's echo: "The bells were the original voice of the cosmos. Before language, before writing, before even the giants — there were bells. And they sang the world into being."
> Lirael (at Tower 6, standing tall, nearly solid): "No. People like *remembering*. They just forgot how."

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Coordinated Global Reset Assault (Final Offensive)
- Reset agents launch simultaneous attacks on multiple towers — their last desperate attempt to prevent grid completion
- Attack composition per tower: ground forces (Reset Enforcers with dissonance jackhammers), aerial drones (anti-resonance chaff deployment), and elite Dissonance Commandos (target bell mechanisms directly)
- The Dissonant One's corruption surges across all zones simultaneously — dark fractal patterns racing along ley lines toward tower positions

**Engagement Style:** Multi-front planetary defense
- Player must triage: which towers need personal intervention vs. which can be held by companions + automated defenses
- Star fort bastions (Moon 4) provide automated turret support for nearby towers
- NPC armies (consolidated from all restored zones) defend secondary positions
- Critical decisions: reinforce a struggling tower (risk losing tuning time) or maintain the synchronization schedule (risk losing a tower)
- The battle happens DURING tuning — combat and music simultaneously, the most demanding gameplay sequence in the entire campaign

### Moon-End Spectacle (Day 28)

**The Planetary Bell-Tower Ring** *(See also: [12_VIVID_VISUALS.md](12_VIVID_VISUALS.md), [14_HAPTIC_FEEDBACK.md](14_HAPTIC_FEEDBACK.md))*

- The most beautiful minute in the game.

> Korath's echo (in the final bell's ring): "I feel the dawn again. Not as memory… as now."

## Moon 13 — Cosmic Moon: True Timeline Convergence

*Theme: Destiny, Truth, Choice, Cosmic Harmony*

### Core Restoration Mechanics

**Echo Realm Travel**
- The 13th Moon rises. The 17th Hour approaches. The sky *trembles*.
- Grid at 95% — the final node lies where it began: beneath New Chicago, in the deepest mud layer yet
- The Aether itself pulls the player downward — the dig is not optional, it's gravitational
- At sufficient depth, the veil ruptures: three parallel timeline instances shimmer into existence:

**Echo Realm 1: The Golden Age**
- The empire at full glory — the most beautiful zone in the game
- Giants walking freely among humans in streets of golden marble
- Airships everywhere — silent flotillas above continent-spanning architecture
- Trains gliding on crystal rails that hum with living song
- Pipe organs thundering 432 Hz symphonies through cathedral corridors of impossible scale
- Children laughing in floating crystal gardens suspended by pure Aether
- Player can walk through this world, interact briefly — but cannot change anything. It is a memory, perfect and heartbreaking.

**Echo Realm 2: The Dissonant Timeline**
- What if Zereth had won — what if the Flood was permanent
- Eternal mud. Silence. Crumbling ruins under a colorless sky
- No song. No light. No life above the surface.
- Corrupted remnants shuffle through the waste — not hostile, just empty
- The player's footsteps are the only sound. The most unsettling zone in the game — beautiful in its desolation.

**Echo Realm 3: The Moment of the Flood**
- The trigger room. Three figures visible at last — in perfect clarity:
  - **Zereth (giant):** Standing at the amplifier controls, radiating 9-Band transcendence energy — his face shows ecstasy, not malice. He is *ascending*, not destroying.
  - **Two humans (Parasite Cabal):** Behind him, hands on secondary controls, reversing the polarity while Zereth is focused on transcendence.
  - **The Truth:** Zereth was experimenting with 9-Band transcendence — trying to evolve the empire beyond physical form. The Cabal infiltrated his lab, reversed the polarity of his star-fort alignment, and used his own technology as the Mud Flood weapon. Zereth tried to stop it — was caught in the blast, frozen in dissonant Aether ice.
  - **He was not the villain. He was the first victim.**

**Resonance Dialogue Combat**
- Final confrontation with Zereth's corrupted echo — not as an enemy to defeat, but as a soul to heal
- The "combat" is harmonic: play resonance sequences to counter his dissonant outbursts
- Match his pain with harmony. Meet his anger with the lullaby Lirael taught you.
- Multi-stage dialogue woven through the harmonic exchange:

> Zereth (breaking, wild dissonance): "I wanted us to become MORE. They took my vision and turned it into a weapon."
> *Player counters with Stone of Dawn harmonic — sunrise chorus*
> Zereth (softening slightly): "All these centuries in the dark, the only voice I heard was my own screaming."
> *Player counters with Lirael's lullaby arrangement*
> Zereth (cracking open): "Do you hear them? The echoes of everyone I failed?"
> *Lirael steps forward, fully solid, singing softly*
> Lirael: "We hear you now."

**Timeline Choice System**
- Three ending paths available — triggered by player's harmonic response during the 13th Moon, 17th Hour:

**Path 1: Harmony (Merge)**
- Forgive Zereth. Channel his transcendence energy WITH the restored grid.
- The Mud Flood reverses in real-time — mud recedes globally, sunken windows rise, buildings emerge in full glory
- The Golden Age and the present merge. Giants walk again. New dawn.

**Path 2: Echo (Preserve)**
- Maintain both timelines as parallel layers — honor the past without erasing the present
- Players can switch between Golden Age and post-Flood realities in post-game
- Zereth finds peace in the between-space, neither fully healed nor fully corrupted

**Path 3: Reset (Control)**
- Side with the Parasite Cabal's philosophy — keep the grid but maintain control over distribution
- Bittersweet: immense power, but the wonder dims. The sky never fully clears.
- The most morally complex choice — power without joy

### Companion Layer

**ALL Companions (Final Personal Arcs)**
- Every companion delivers their culminating emotional beat:
  - **Milo:** Drops the merchant act entirely. Stands clean, vulnerable, real.
  - **Lirael:** Fully manifested — solid, grown, voice clear. She remembers everything.
  - **Thorne:** Lands the flagship beside the player. Military salute — first genuine sign of respect.
  - **Korath's Echo:** His voice in the wind, barely a whisper — but steady, warm, proud.
  - **Veritas:** Plays the final note of the unfinished Requiem — the piece that was interrupted by the Flood.
  - **Adopted children:** Grown, skilled, standing tall at their stations across the grid.
  - **Cassian (if redeemed):** Provides the final key to the trigger room — his knowledge completes the truth.
  - **Cassian (if purged):** Ghost howls during the convergence — an echo of what could have been.
  - **Zereth:** Healed (Harmony), at peace (Echo), or silent (Reset).

**Final Companion Dialogue (Harmony Path — Canon):**

> Lirael (singing the lullaby from Moon 1 — but now the whole city joins)
> Milo (standing clean for the first time in the game, tears in his eyes): "To forgetting less, and remembering more."
> Thorne (landing his flagship, saluting): "The sky's ours again, spark."
> Korath's echo (in the wind): "The song resumes."
> Zereth (healed, standing beside his brother's light): "At last."

### Combat Framework

**Enemy Type:** Dissonant Manifestations (cosmic-scale) + Temporal Fragments
- Dissonant Manifestations: the accumulated corruption of centuries given form — enormous, planet-threatening entities that emerge from the rupture between timelines
- Temporal Fragments: echoes of every enemy type from all 12 previous Moons — a greatest-hits gauntlet testing mastery of all combat forms
- Final Boss (sort of): Zereth's corrupted shell — not destroyed by combat but dissolved by harmonic dialogue. Force cannot win; only resonance prevails.

**Engagement Style:** Total war + harmonic resolution
- Every combat system active simultaneously: ground forces, airship fleet, train-mounted cannons, bell tower area attacks, fountain purification, giant-mode Titan fighting
- The battle is the framework for the harmonic dialogue — fighting and healing happen in the same breath
- Companions actively fight, each using their signature abilities — the full party united for the first and only time
- Resolution: when the player's harmonic dialogue reaches Zereth, the combat enemies lose coherence — they don't die, they simply *stop being wrong*

**The True Timeline Convergence** *(See also: [12_VIVID_VISUALS.md](12_VIVID_VISUALS.md), [14_HAPTIC_FEEDBACK.md](14_HAPTIC_FEEDBACK.md))*
