## 1. Campaign Architecture

The campaign unfolds across **13 Moons** (28 in-game days each), matching the restored Tartarian 13-Moon calendar. Each Moon is simultaneously:
- A **narrative chapter** advancing the main story
- A **mechanical expansion** introducing new systems
- A **cosmic event** with unique Aether modifiers
- A **live-ops season** for recurring engagement

**Total campaign length:** ~3–4 months of daily play (5–15 min sessions)  
**Replayability:** Branching choices create 4+ distinct playthroughs; post-game sandbox is infinite

---

## 2. Structural Symmetry & Asymmetry

### Symmetry (Every Moon follows this rhythm)

Each Moon has exactly **5 beats**, creating reliable pacing players can feel:

| Beat | Phase | Duration (approx) |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Discovery** | New zone/mechanic revealed | Days 1–5 |
| **2. Restoration** | Major architecture revival + tuning | Days 6–12 |
| **3. Conflict** | Rising dissonance or Reset interference | Days 13–18 |
| **4. Climax** | Spectacular cosmic event or battle | Days 19–24 |
| **5. Revelation** | Deep lore drop + crossover ripple | Days 25–28 |

### Asymmetry (No two Moons feel the same)

| Dimension | How It Varies |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Ranges from pure wonder (Moon 5) to heartbreak (Moon 7) to triumph (Moon 13) |
| **Scale** | Intimate single-building focus (Moon 1) to planetary scope (Moon 12) |
| **Mechanic** | Each Moon introduces exactly ONE new system (never two) |
| **Threat Level** | Golems (early) → Reset agents (mid) → Dissonant One (late) |
| **Companion Focus** | Rotating spotlight: Milo (1), Cassian (2), Lirael (3), Korath (7), Thorne (8) |
| **Emotional Color** | Wonder → challenge → tenderness → mystery → awe → grief → transcendence |
| **Aether Band Focus** | 3-band (1–4) → 6-band (5–8) → 9-band (9–13) |

## 3. Crossover Web

Every Moon plants **seeds** that bloom in later Moons and **callbacks** to earlier ones. No storyline exists in isolation.

### Forward Seeds (planted → harvested)

```
Moon 1 (Spire fragment)     ──────────→ Moon 5 (White City full spire)
Moon 1 (Lirael appears)     ──────────→ Moon 3 (Lirael's orphan truth)
                             ──────────→ Moon 7 (Lirael meets Korath)

Moon 2 (Cassian hints)      ──────────→ Moon 7 (Cassian confrontation OR redemption)

Moon 8 (Airship armada)     ──────────→ Moon 10 (Trains + airships combined transport)
```

### Backward Callbacks (referencing earlier events)

Every Moon from 4 onward contains:
- At least ONE Milo comment referencing a previous Moon's event
- At least ONE zone upgrade visible from the previous Moon's restoration
- At least ONE NPC whose behavior changed based on earlier player choices

## 4. Prologue: The First Note Beneath the Mud

*This plays once, before Moon 1 begins. ~3 minutes. Sets the tone for the entire experience.*

**Setting:** A rain-lashed night in "New Chicago." The sky is choked and gray. Buildings are half-sunk. Nobody notices.

**The Moment:** During the hidden 17th hour of the 13th Moon — the most powerful temporal alignment in the Tartarian calendar — a faint mercury finial barely protrudes from the mud near your apartment.

**The Vision (Cinematic):**
- Giants singing frequencies that cut granite like warm butter
- Airships with sacred-geometry hulls floating megaliths through aurora-lit night
- Silent Aether trains gliding on copper rails that hum with living song
- Cathedrals with massive pipe organs thundering 432 Hz while pure water fountains spray ionized mist into floating auroras
- Bell towers ringing scalar waves across continents beneath the 13 Moons
- Everything — stone, water, sky, blood — breathing as one instrument

Then the vision *fractures*. Darkness. The Mud Flood. The sound of the world drowning in silence.

**The Awakening:**
You open your eyes, glowing mud on your hands. The finial pulses with faint 3-band light.

**First Dialogue:**  
*Aether whisper:* "The song was never lost. It was only buried. Will you remember how to listen?"  
*Milo (appearing from behind a dumpster):* "You look like you just saw ghosts in top hats dancing on airships. Welcome to the 'modern' world, spark."

### MOON 1: MAGNETIC MOON — "The Pull of Awakening"
**Galactic Tone:** Purpose / Initiation  
**Aether Modifier:** +20% excavation yield  
**New Mechanic:** Excavation + first dome restoration + 60-second giant-mode bursts  
**Companion Focus:** Milo (introduction) + Lirael (first appearance)  
**Zones:** New Chicago underground (1 zone)

First resonance scan reveals buried cathedral. Swipe-excavation tutorial — satisfying reveal of precision-cut stone behind layers of hardened mud. Sunken windows everywhere; Milo sells "antique" bricks: "Genuine 1893 mud brick! Only slightly cursed!"

Inside the cathedral: a decaying massive pipe organ, pipes still faintly humming 432 Hz. Pure water font cracked but still damp.

**Lore Fragment:** Excavated stone carved with 3-6-9 sequence and golden-ratio diagrams — clearly not Victorian.

#### Restoration (Days 6–12)
First tuning mini-game: simple 3-note organ sequence. Perfect tune = dome glows, rose window projects cymatic pattern onto the floor, pure water font trickles back to life.

Place first spire on dome. Visual: blue-white sparks climb it at night. First ley-line vein lights up on the mini-map — golden thread pointing toward... something vast in the distance.

**Milo:** "Blimey… that thing just lit up like someone flipped a switch in the sky. I've been selling 'antique' bricks for years and none of 'em ever glowed."

#### Conflict (Days 13–18)
Reset scouts arrive — Victorian-costumed goons with clipboards and jackhammers. Tutorial combat: harmonic staff blasts + basic dodge.

First giant-mode burst: skeleton in the mud pulses with your bloodline. For 60 exhilarating seconds, you're 15 feet tall. Toss the scouts into a mud pit. It feels *incredible*.

#### Climax (Days 19–24)
The Buried Beacon: mercury-ball spire clutched in a giant's skeletal hand. Tune it during a 17th-hour alignment → the cathedral erupts with light. Ley lines spread outward, lighting distant spires you can't reach yet.

#### Revelation (Days 25–28)
**Lirael appears** — translucent, humming a lullaby in 432 Hz. She doesn't remember her name, only the song. She asks: "Why do grown-ups build houses then live in the attic?"

**Deep Lore:** Skeleton hums reveal first prophecy fragment: a figure in shadow (the Dissonant One) standing atop a star fort, reaching for something in the sky.

**Crossover Seeds Planted:**
- Spire fragment (blooms in Moon 5)
- Lirael's lullaby (key to Moon 3 + Moon 6)
- Giant skeleton key #1 (collect 8 total)
- Airship component fragment (blooms in Moon 8)

**Aether Band Focus:** 9-Band prophecy / timeline access

#### Discovery (Days 1–5)
Prophecy stones — ancient crystals inscribed with golden-ratio patterns — begin appearing across the grid, visible as floating golden markers. Each is hidden at a ley-line intersection, accessible only via airship or train.

**If Cassian was redeemed (Moon 7):** He provides coded translations from his Reset contact days, speeding stone discovery.  
**If Cassian was purged:** His ghost-echo still haunts stone locations, offering cryptic directions.

**Milo (staring up):** "That's real, isn't it? Not a sales pitch. Not a postcard. That's what we were supposed to have."

Then it fades. And the loss hits harder than any boss fight.

The Mud Flood happened *after* the vision ends. What happened between the ringing bells and the cataclysm?

**17-Hour Clock Tower:** Prophetic instructions hidden inside Stone 4 lead to a complete 17-hour clock mechanism. Install it in a bell tower → permanent time-bend ability.

**Crossover Seeds:**

Excavate the aquifer. Channel purified water through precision-cut tunnels (giant mode) into a network of underground pipes connecting every restored zone. Surface fountains activate in chain reaction — one after another, city by city.

Each activated fountain sprays ionized mist that heals everything in its radius: structures gain RS, NPCs gain health, Echo remnants become more solid.

#### Conflict (Days 13–18)
Corrupted water sources fight back — sentient black-sludge tendrils (Mud Flood remnants with vestigial intelligence) clog pipes and attack. Cleanse with 6-band resonance + fountain water counter-pressure.

**Milo (knee-deep in sludge):** "I've sold mud, built on mud, lived in mud — and I STILL hate this stuff."

Planetary fountain activation: every fountain on every continent sprays simultaneously. Ionized mist creates **continent-wide aurora veils** — shimmering prismatic curtains visible from space (if you were in an airship, which you are).

**Thorne (from the airship bridge, looking down):** "The old world had a word for this. Kairos. The moment when everything aligns and the universe exhales."

#### Revelation (Days 25–28)
**Lore Revelation:** The pure water was the true lifeblood of the empire — not just for drinking but for conducting Aether, healing cellular damage, and maintaining the resonance sensitivity that allowed human-giant cooperation. The Reset's first strategic target was the aquifer system.

Prophecy Stones 10–11 appear: Stone of Healing, Stone of Warning. The Warning stone shows the first tremors — **and three figures at the trigger device**.

**Crossover Seeds:**

The global grid hits **95%**. One more connection remains.

### MOON 13: COSMIC MOON — "The Presence of Enduring"
**Galactic Tone:** Endurance / Transcendence  
**Aether Modifier:** ALL bands at maximum potential  
**New Mechanic:** Echo realm travel + True Timeline merge  
**Companion Focus:** ALL companions + The Dissonant One (Zereth)  
**Zones:** The Final Node + Echo Realms (3 parallel timeline zones)  
**Aether Band Focus:** TRANSCENDENT (beyond 9-band)

#### Discovery (Days 1–5)
The 13th Moon rises. The 17th Hour approaches. The sky *trembles*.

Your global grid is at 95% — one final node remains, located where it all began: beneath New Chicago, in the deepest mud layer you've ever encountered. But this isn't just another dig. The Aether itself is pulling you downward.

**Zereth's voice (clearer now, less distorted):** "You've almost done what I could not. Connected what I tore apart. But before you finish… you deserve the truth."

#### Restoration (Days 6–12)
Enter the **Echo Realms** — parallel timeline instances that shimmer into existence as the 13th Moon peaks:

**Echo Realm 1: Golden Age (the world before the Flood)**  
The empire at full glory. Giants walking freely. Airships everywhere. Silent trains crossing continents. Pipe organs thundering. Children laughing in floating gardens. Pure water fountains on every corner. The most beautiful zone in the game.

**Echo Realm 2: The Dissonant Timeline (what if Zereth won)**  
Eternal mud. Silence. Crumbling ruins under a colorless sky. No song. No light. The cost of failure made tangible.

**Echo Realm 3: The Moment of the Flood**  
You witness it. The trigger room. Three figures: Zereth (giant), and two humans from the Parasite Cabal. The truth unfolds:

> Zereth was experimenting with 9-band transcendence — trying to evolve the empire beyond physical form. The Cabal infiltrated his lab, reversed the polarity of his star-fort alignment, and used his own technology as the Mud Flood weapon. Zereth tried to stop it — and was caught in the blast, frozen in dissonant Aether ice.

**He was not the villain. He was the first victim.**

#### Conflict (Days 13–18)
Final confrontation with Zereth's corrupted echo — not as a villain to defeat, but as a tormented soul caught between rage and grief for centuries. The "combat" is a **resonance dialogue**: play harmonic sequences to counter his dissonant outbursts. Match his pain with harmony. Meet his anger with the lullaby Lirael taught you.

**Zereth (breaking):** "I wanted us to become MORE. They took my vision and turned it into a weapon. All these centuries in the dark, the only voice I heard was my own screaming."

**Lirael (stepping forward, fully solid, singing softly):** "We hear you now."

#### Climax (Days 19–24)
The final node activation during the **13th Moon, 17th Hour** — the same cosmic alignment as the prologue.

All companions present. Every ley line lit. Every bell ringing. Every organ thundering. Every fountain spraying. Every train humming. Every airship circling.

**THE CHOICE:**

1. **Harmony Path (Merge):** Forgive Zereth. Channel his transcendence energy *with* the restored grid. The Mud Flood reverses in real time — mud recedes globally, sunken windows rise, buildings emerge in full glory. The Golden Age and the present merge. Giants walk again. New dawn.

2. **Echo Path (Preserve):** Maintain both timelines as parallel layers. Players can switch between Golden Age and post-Flood realities in post-game. Zereth finds peace in the between-space.

3. **Reset Path (Control):** Side with the Parasite Cabal's philosophy — keep the grid but maintain control over its distribution. Bittersweet: immense power, but the wonder dims. The sky never fully clears.

#### Revelation (Days 25–28)
**Harmony Path Ending (Canon):**

The mud recedes. Sunken windows rise. Airships fill the sky. Resonance trains glide silently. Bell towers ring in perpetual harmony. Pipe organs thunder 432 Hz symphonies while pure water fountains spray ionized mist under the 13 Moons. Giants walk among humans again. The 17-hour clocks are restored. The 13 Moons glow in their true sequence.

**Final Scene:**

You stand atop the tallest restored spire — the one you first touched in the prologue. But now it's complete: golden, radiant, singing.

**Lirael (fully manifested, grown, voice clear):** Sings the lullaby from Moon 1. But now the whole city joins.

**Milo (standing clean for the first time in the game, tears in his eyes):** "To forgetting less, and remembering more."

**Zereth (healed, standing beside his brother's light):** "At last."
