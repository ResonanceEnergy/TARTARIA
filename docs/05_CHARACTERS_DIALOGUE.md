## 1. Dialogue Design Philosophy

### Core Rules

1. **No Exposition Dumps.** NPCs reveal lore through personality, not textbooks. Korath doesn't lecture about the Golden Age — he misses it. Milo doesn't explain the Reset — he profits from it.
2. **Personality First.** Every line could only be said by THAT character. Swap a Milo line into Thorne's mouth and it should feel immediately wrong.
3. **Wit Over Weight.** Even heavy moments have a glimmer of humor or warmth. This isn't grimdark — it's bittersweet wonder.
4. **Player Never Speaks.** Elara is a silent protagonist. NPCs react to player actions, choices, and expressions (communicated through gameplay) — not dialogue trees with player lines.
5. **Short & Punchy.** Mobile format: 2 sentences max per dialogue bubble. No scrolling walls of text. If it can't be said in a breath, split it.
6. **Callbacks Are Currency.** Companions remember. Moon 8 Milo should reference Moon 2 events naturally.

### Tone Spectrum

```
Wonder ─────── Humor ─────── Tenderness ─────── Mystery ─────── Grief ─────── Awe
  Lirael         Milo          Korath            Zereth         Thorne       All (Moon 13)
```

## 2. Main Cast Profiles

---

### 2.1 ELARA VOSS — The Resonance Spark (Player Character)

**Role:** Silent protagonist, last of the Tartarian bloodline  
**Age:** 20s  
**Height:** Human-sized (grows to 15ft in giant mode)  
**Visual:** Practical modern clothes that gradually incorporate Tartarian elements (copper bracers, resonance crystal pendant, golden-ratio tattoo emerging on forearm)  
**Voice:** Silent — emotes through animations, Aether-glow intensity, and companion reactions  

**Character Arc:**
- Moon 1: Confused, newly awakened
- Moon 4: Confident restorer
- Moon 7: Leader carrying weight of responsibility
- Moon 10: Networker connecting entire continents
- Moon 13: Transcendent figure standing between worlds

**Design Note:** Elara's silence makes the player the voice. NPCs project meaning onto her. Milo sees a partner in crime. Korath sees a grandchild. Lirael sees a mother. Thorne sees a commander. The player becomes all of these.

### 2.2 MILO THE MUDSLINGER — Comic Relief / Heart

**Role:** Companion (Moon 1 onward), former black-market antiquities dealer  
**Age:** 30s  
**Height:** 5'8" (constantly aware of how short he is next to giants)  
**Visual:** Shabby-chic — tweed vest over a band t-shirt, mud-caked boots, fingerless gloves, always carrying a satchel overflowing with dubiously sourced "artifacts"  
**Voice:** Quick, East London-ish, self-deprecating, always selling something even when nobody's buying  

**Personality Core:** Milo uses humor as armor. Beneath the wisecracks is someone who's been alone too long, selling fake history because real history was taken from him. His arc is about discovering that the real thing is better than the knockoff.

**Character Arc:**
| Moon | State | Voice Shift |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cynical scammer | "Everything's for sale, spark" |
| 3 | Reluctantly moved by orphan train | Jokes get quieter |
| 5 | Genuinely angry at White City demolition | First sincere outburst |
| 7 | Witness to Korath's sacrifice | Goes silent for first time |
| 10 | Continental train ride pride | "Maybe I'm a builder, not a seller" |
| 13 | Standing clean, tears, transformed | "To forgetting less" |

**Signature Phrases:**
- "Genuine Tartarian [X]! Only slightly cursed!"
- "I've seen things… mostly in catalogues, but still"
- "Is this the part where we run? It feels like the running part"
- *When player builds something beautiful:* "Right, even I'm impressed. And I'm professionally jaded"

### 2.3 LIRAEL — The Echo Child

**Role:** Companion (Moon 1 onward), spectral orphan from the Mud Flood  
**Age:** Appears 8–10 (actually centuries old)  
**Height:** Child-sized, translucent, faintly glowing  
**Visual:** Victorian-era orphan dress, but made of shimmering Aether light. Hair floats slightly. Eyes are too old for her face.  
**Voice:** Musical, wondering, occasionally heartbreaking — speaks in half-questions that sound like song lyrics  

**Personality Core:** Lirael isn't a ghost — she's an Echo, a fragment of consciousness preserved in Aether. She remembers feelings and songs but not facts. She asks questions nobody else thinks to ask, and her simple observations often contain profound truth.

**Character Arc:**
| Moon | State | Physicality |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Whisper, barely visible | Translucent shimmer |
| 3 | Remembers the orphan train | Slightly more solid |
| 6 | Conducts children's choir | Voice grows strong |
| 7 | Learns Korath's songs | Can touch objects briefly |
| 11 | Fountain healing makes her semi-solid | Cast shadows |
| 13 | Fully manifested, grown | Solid, radiant, voice clear |

**Signature Phrases:**
- "Why do grown-ups build houses then live in the attic?"
- "The song's breaking…" (when dissonance appears)
- "I remember this feeling but not the name for it"
- "Can you hear the stones? They're humming behind their teeth"
- *When something beautiful is restored:* "There. The world just took a breath."

### 2.4 CAPTAIN THORNE — The Enduring Pilot

**Role:** Companion (radio Moon 5, in-person Moon 8 onward), legendary Tartarian airship captain  
**Age:** Centuries old (appears 50s, weathered)  
**Height:** 7'2" (tall for a human — "giant-adjacent" bloodline)  
**Visual:** Tartarian flight coat (long, copper-buttoned, slightly too large), goggles pushed up on forehead, silver-streaked beard, calloused hands that know every rivet of his ship  
**Voice:** Gravel and patience — speaks like someone who's had too much time and not enough conversation  

**Personality Core:** Thorne has been circling the sky for two centuries, alone, keeping his fleet alive through sheer stubbornness. He lost everything — crew, passengers, world — and survived by refusing to land in a world that wasn't worth landing in. Your restoration gives him a reason.

**Character Arc:**
| Moon | State | Emotion |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Distant radio crackle | Cautious hope |
| 8 | Lands, joins party | Gruff relief |
| 10 | Continental train pride | Quiet awe |
| 12 | Fleet in formation | Stoic tears |
| 13 | Salutes at finale | Peace |

**Signature Phrases:**
- "Two centuries circling on stale air and stubbornness"
- "This bucket flies like it's still offended we dug it out of the mud"
- "I've seen horizons you haven't built yet"
- *When the player builds something grand:* "Now THAT'S worth landing for"
- *Mid-combat:* "Hold tight, spark. We're about to remind these parasites what small feels like"
- *Quiet moment:* "The old world had a word for this: Kairos. The moment when everything aligns and the universe exhales"

### 2.5 KORATH THE BUILDER — The Ancient Mentor

**Role:** Companion (Moon 7–7, then echo forever), last awakened Tartarian giant  
**Age:** Thousands of years  
**Height:** 25 feet  
**Visual:** Weathered obsidian skin with golden veins (Aether circuits), kind eyes set deep in a craggy face, hands scarred from centuries of building. Wears a simple stone-worker's apron. Moves slowly but with absolute precision.  
**Voice:** Deep and warm — rumbles like distant drums wrapped in velvet. Speaks carefully, each word placed like a stone in a wall.  

**Personality Core:** Korath is grief and grace made enormous. He lost both brothers — Maelix to corruption, Zereth to madness (or so he believes). He went into stasis hoping to wake in a better world. Instead he wakes to you — small, imperfect, trying hard. And he decides that's enough.

**Character Arc:**
| Moon | State | Teaching |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | Foreshadowed (inscription) | — |
| 7 | Awakened, teaches, sacrifices | Rock cutting, 9-band |
| 8+ | Echo in resonance systems | Guidance during key moments |
| 12 | Voice in the final bell | "I feel the dawn again" |
| 13 | Wind-whisper at convergence | "The song resumes" |

**Signature Phrases:**
- "You are trying. That is everything."
- "Do not force the line. Whisper to it. The golden spiral remembers its own name."
- "They've learned nothing! Stone does not forget its song!"
- "Do not mourn the pause, child. Celebrate the resumption. Sing louder than the silence ever was."
- *Looking at your small restoration:* "I built mountains. You build meaning. Yours is harder."
- *About Zereth:* "He did not want destruction. He wanted transcendence. But the cosmos does not grant wishes to the impatient."

### 2.6 CASSIAN — The Ambiguous One

**Role:** Conditional ally/antagonist (Moon 2 intro, Moon 7 resolution)  
**Age:** 30s  
**Height:** 6'1"  
**Visual:** Immaculately dressed in a blend of modern and Tartarian — sharp coat, resonance-crystal cufflinks, too-clean shoes for someone in the mud. Hair always perfect. Smiles too easily.  
**Voice:** Smooth, educated, charming — the kind of voice that sells you something while taking something else  

**Personality Core:** Cassian genuinely believes the Reset was necessary — not out of malice but out of pragmatism. "Free energy sounds noble until kings become obsolete." He's the intellectual antagonist: not evil, just wrong (maybe). His redemption depends on whether the player shows him wonder is worth more than order.

**Branching Dialogue:**

**If TRUSTED (Moon 2–7):**
| Moon | Line |
|---|---|
| 2 | "These crystals are Reset sabotage. I've seen them before. Let me help." |
| 5 | "The White City… I'd read about it. Reports never mentioned the light." |
| 7 (Betrayal) | "I'm sorry. Truly. But you can't bring back what wasn't sustainable." |
| 7 (If redeemed) | *Tears.* "Show me. Show me it's real and not just… beautiful noise." |

**If DOUBTED (Moon 2–7):**
| Moon | Line |
|---|---|
| 2 | "Oh, you don't trust me? Smart. Most people aren't." |
| 5 | *Caught studying your blueprints.* "Just admiring the craftsmanship." |
| 7 (Confrontation) | "Free energy makes kings obsolete. I chose order. What did you choose?" |
| 7 (If purged) | "You'll remember this mercy was offered." *Fades into dissonance.* |

### 2.7 ZERETH — The Dissonant One (Revealed)

**Role:** Central mystery figure, final act presence  
**Age:** Same as Korath (millennia)  
**Height:** 28 feet (slightly taller than Korath)  
**Visual:** Once identical to Korath in build. Now: fractured obsidian skin leaking violet-black dissonance energy, one eye dark, one eye still showing the golden Aether glow he once had. His hands — builder's hands — shake.  
**Voice:** Distorted, layered, agonized — like a beautiful chord played through shattered speakers  

**Personality Core:** Zereth was the most gifted of the three brothers — the one who pushed the boundaries of Aether into transcendence. He wasn't the villain. The Cabal used his experiment as a weapon. He's been screaming in the dark for centuries, and the scream has become his identity. Meeting the player is the first time someone has listened instead of fought.

**Dialogue appears only in Moons 9, 12, 13:**

| Moon | Line |
|---|---|
| 9 | "You see paradise. I saw a cage. They called it harmony. I called it submission." |
| 12 | *During planetary bell sync, barely audible:* "The bells… I remember composing for them…" |
| 13 | "I wanted us to become MORE. They took my vision and turned it into a weapon." |
| 13 | *Breaking:* "All these centuries in the dark, the only voice I heard was my own screaming." |
| 13 (If healed) | "At last." *Two words. Centuries of weight released.* |

## 3. Companion Dialogue Systems

### 3.1 Idle Chatter (Passive)

When exploring with companions, they comment every 60–90 seconds on context:

**Trigger Categories:**
1. **Location-based** — different lines for each zone
2. **Time-based** — different lines for day/night/17th Hour
3. **Progress-based** — different lines at 25%/50%/75%/100% zone RS
4. **Weather-based** — rain, storm, clear, fog
5. **Companion-combo** — unique lines when specific companions are together

### 3.2 Proximity Banter (Two+ Companions)

When companions are near each other, they converse:

**Milo + Lirael:**
> **Milo:** "So… you're a ghost?"  
> **Lirael:** "Are you alive? You never seem sure."  
> **Milo:** "…fair point, actually."

**Milo + Thorne:**
> **Milo:** "Captain, what's the most valuable thing on your ship?"  
> **Thorne:** "The view."  
> **Milo:** "I meant something I could sell—"  
> **Thorne:** "I know what you meant."

**Lirael + Korath (Moon 7):**
> **Lirael:** "You're big. Were all the builders big?"  
> **Korath:** "We were tall. The children were the builders. We just… reached the high parts."  
> **Lirael:** *giggles* "I want to reach the high parts."

**Milo + Korath:**
> **Milo:** "So be honest, big fella — how much gold's in these walls?"  
> **Korath:** "More than currency. More than weight. Enough to change a world."  
> **Milo:** "…Right, but like, in actual money—"  
> **Korath:** *long stare*  
> **Milo:** "Never mind."

**Thorne + Korath (Moon 7):**
> **Thorne:** "I kept your ships flying. Two centuries. Mostly duct tape."  
> **Korath:** "I know. I heard the engines from my ice. Stubborn little captain."  
> **Thorne:** "'Little.' Right."

### 3.3 Milestone Reactions

When player completes major building achievements:

**First Dome Restored:**
> **Milo:** "Blimey… that thing just lit up like someone flipped a switch in the sky."  
> **Lirael:** "There. The world just took a breath."

**First Star Fort Completed:**
> **Milo:** "It's a star! On the ground! I could sell maps to this—"  
> **Lirael:** "It's not for selling. It's for holding. Can you feel it hold?"

**First Continental Train Ride:**
> **Thorne:** "In my day, the trains ran at the speed of song. Yours are slower. But they've got more heart."  
> **Milo:** *looking out window at restored zones* "We built all that? Me included?"  
> **Lirael:** "The world is singing from the windows."

**Cathedral at 100 RS:**
> **Korath (echo):** "This is what I remember. This is what I froze myself hoping to find."  
> **Milo:** *quiet, staring up* "…I've got nothing. For once. I've just got nothing."

## 4. NPC Banter Library

### 4.1 Milo's Greatest Hits (Contextual)

**During Excavation:**
- "Careful! That's either a priceless artifact or a really old boot. Fifty-fifty."
- "I once sold a 'genuine Tartarian teacup' that turned out to be a toilet. Made six hundred quid."
- "If we find gold, I get thirty percent. Non-negotiable. …Okay, twenty. Fifteen?"

**During Combat:**
- "Is this the part where we run? It feels like the running part."
- "I'm not a fighter! I'm a *merchant*! …Fine, an *armed* merchant."
- "Remember when this was just digging up pretty rocks? I miss that."

**During Building:**
- "I can't believe I'm saying this but… could you make it taller? It deserves to be taller."
- "Even I'm impressed. And I'm professionally jaded."
- "This is either the most beautiful thing I've ever seen or I've been in the mud too long."

**During Quiet Moments:**
- "Do you think they hoped someone would find all this? Or did they think it was over?"
- "My gran used to say the old buildings had souls. I thought she was barmy. I owe her an apology."
- "Sometimes at night I look at what you've built and I think… yeah. This is better than selling it."

**During Moon 5 (White City):**
- "They bulldozed this?! For a parking lot and a hot dog stand?! I've been selling the wrong history…"
- "Every piece of marble here is worth more than everything I've ever sold combined. And I mean the *idea* of it, not the geological survey value."

**During Moon 7 (Korath's sacrifice):**
- *Extended silence. First time Milo has nothing to say.*
- Eventually: "The big guy… he deserved more than this. They all did."

### 4.2 Lirael's Observations

**On Architecture:**
- "The dome is smiling. Can you see it? When you tuned it right, it smiled."
- "The columns hold the sky up. Not literally. But also literally."
- "Why do people build walls? The giants never needed walls."

**On the Mud Flood:**
- "They told us the mud was a blanket. It was a grave."
- "I remember the sound. Not the mud. The silence after. That was worse."
- "The water remembers what it tasted like before the mud. It wants to come home."

**On Companions:**
- *About Milo:* "He pretends everything is for sale. Even himself. But some things don't have prices."
- *About Thorne:* "The captain's heart is an engine. It runs on 'not yet.'"
- *About Korath:* "He's bigger than the buildings but smaller than his sadness."
- *About Zereth:* "His voice sounds like music that forgot the words."

**On the Player:**
- "You carry the old fire. It's small but it's the right color."
- "When you build, the stones remember how to fit. You're not making — you're reminding."
- "Will you sing when this is over? Or will you just listen?"

### 4.3 Thorne's Gruff Wisdom

**On Flying:**
- "The sky doesn't forget good pilots. It just makes you wait."
- "Mercury engines don't run on fuel. They run on belief. And a healthy disrespect for gravity."
- "Little ones on my bridge. Wonderful. Now I need child-sized railings. And more patience."

**On the Old World:**
- "I remember when every city had a heartbeat you could hear from ten thousand feet."
- "They didn't build roads. They built songs and walked along them."
- "The air used to taste like copper and kindness. Now it just tastes like Tuesday."

**On Combat:**
- "Hold tight, spark. We're about to remind these parasites what it feels like to be small."
- "Two centuries of patience just ran out."
- "I've been dodging storms since before their grandparents were myths."

**On Beauty (Restored Zones):**
- "Look at that. Rivers of light from here to the edge of the world. Makes a captain almost believe in endings that aren't tragic."
- "Now THAT'S worth landing for."
- *Looking at a continental view:* "This. This is what I stayed in the sky for."

### 4.4 Korath's Foundational Truths

**On Building:**
- "Do not force the line. Whisper to it. The golden spiral remembers its own name. Let it draw itself."
- "I built mountains. You build meaning. Yours is harder."
- "Every stone is a word. A wall is a sentence. A city is a story. What are you writing?"

**On Loss:**
- "I went into the ice expecting dawn. I woke to twilight. But you — you are the first light."
- "Maelix, my brother. I built the inscription with my own hands. I should have built a shield instead."
- "The cosmos does not grant wishes to the impatient."

**On the Player:**
- "You are trying. That is everything."
- "Small hands. Great stubbornness. Yes, you remind me of the best of us."
- "When I am gone, the stones will still know my touch. They will know yours too."

## 5. Echo NPCs & Spectral Voices

### 5.1 Echo Types

| Type | Visual | Behavior | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Residual** | Faint shimmer, repeating one action | Loop forever; provide ambient lore | Giant stonemason cutting endlessly |
| **Conversational** | Semi-solid, respond to proximity | Speak one remembered phrase | "The trains ran on time. On song, actually." |
| **Quest** | Nearly solid, have needs | Give side quests, reveal lore | "Find my tuning fork — I dropped it when the mud came" |
| **Choir** | Groups of children, singing | Join Lirael's choir when found | Sing fragments of 432 Hz melodies |

### 5.2 Sample Echo Dialogues

> "The frequency is 174 for granite. 285 for marble. Never use 666. …Never use 666."  
> *Repeats endlessly. Warning about dissonant frequency.*

**Train Conductor Echo (Moon 3 zone):**
> "All aboard! Next stop: everywhere!" *laughs*  
> "We connected the world in a single rail. They called it impossible. We called it Tuesday."

**Bell Ringer Echo (Moon 6 zone):**

## 6. Antagonist Dialogue

### 6.1 Reset Agents

**Generic Reset Agent (combat trigger):**
- "This structure is classified as temporary exhibit 47-B. Cease unauthorized restoration."
- "You are in violation of Historical Continuity Protocol. Stand down."
- "Progress requires forgetting. That's not cruelty — it's efficiency."

**Reset Commander (Moon 12 transmission):**
- "You can't just… bring it all back. People like order. They like forgetting."
- "We didn't destroy your world. We replaced it with one that works."
- "Every empire falls. We just made sure this one stayed down."

### 6.2 Mud Golems

Golems don't speak — they **groan** in inverted frequencies. But corrupted giant golems retain fragments:

**Corrupted Golem (Moon 4):**
> "The song… the song was… WRONG…" *attacks*

**Maelix's Golem (final moments):**
> "Brother… I held the line… the line held me…" *crumbles*

### 6.3 The Parasite Cabal (Ambient)

Never seen directly — only through diary fragments, coded letters, and whispered transmissions:

> "The aquifer feeds everything. Salt the source and the tree dies at the crown."  
> "Pavilion 7 through 12: demolish by March 1894. Classify as temporary plaster."  
> "The children remember. Separate and scatter. Twenty years will erase what centuries could not."  
> "Controlership memorandum: The word 'Tartaria' is to be removed from all maps by 1906."

## 8. Dialogue Triggers & Contextual Lines

### 8.1 Trigger Matrix

| Trigger | Who Speaks | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| First excavation (any zone) | Milo | High |
| First combat | Milo | High |
| First giant-mode activation | Lirael | High |
| First building at 100 RS | Korath (echo) | Critical |
| First airship flight | Thorne | Critical |
| Dissonance appears | Lirael (warning) | High |
| NPC death/crumble | Milo or Thorne | Critical |
| Zone-wide event (aurora, flood) | All companions react | Medium |
| Player idle >30 seconds | Random companion (idle chatter) | Low |
| 17th Hour begins | Aether whisper | Medium |
| Moon transition | Narrative voiceover | Critical |

### 8.2 Anti-Repetition System

- Each companion has **30+ unique idle lines per zone**
- Lines are flagged as "played" and cooldown for 24 real-time hours
- Critical story lines never repeat; idle chatter recycles after full rotation
- If two companions would speak simultaneously, priority NPC wins + second NPC gets a reaction line instead

### 8.3 Callback System

Companions track major player choices and reference them:

**If player redeemed Cassian (Moon 7+):**
> **Milo (Moon 10):** "Still can't believe you forgave the suit. He's actually useful now though. Annoyingly."
> **Thorne (Moon 11):** "Your mercy gave him a chance. My patience is giving him a deadline."

**If player purged Cassian:**
> **Milo (Moon 10):** "Sometimes I think about the suit. Then I remember what he did. Then I don't."
> **Lirael (Moon 11):** "His echo still walks the corridors. He pretends he's not there. I pretend I don't see him."

## 9. Voice Direction Notes

### 9.1 Casting Guidelines

| Character | Voice Type | Reference Tone | Key Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Milo** | Quick, warm tenor | Simon Pegg meets a London estate agent | Never mean, always selling — even sincerity |
| **Lirael** | Musical child soprano | Pan's Labyrinth's Ofelia | Timeless innocence, not naiveté |
| **Thorne** | Deep, weathered bass | Idris Elba with more patience | Every word earned, nothing wasted |
| **Korath** | Rolling, subharmonic bass | James Earl Jones if he were singing | Gentle but tectonic — you feel him before you hear him |
| **Cassian** | Polished, confident baritone | Tom Hiddleston with more edge | Charisma concealing conviction |
| **Zereth** | Distorted, layered tenor/bass | Cate Blanchett's Galadriel + digital corruption | Beautiful voice destroyed, fighting to be heard |
| **Reset Agents** | Flat, bureaucratic tenor | Every DMV clerk merged into one | Menacing through boredom |
| **Aether Whispers** | Sexless, harmonic, multi-layered | Wind chime meets distant choir | Not a person — a frequency that happens to form words |

### 9.2 Performance Notes

**For Milo:** Never pause before a joke. The humor should feel reflexive, not prepared. When he's sincere, he should sound surprised by his own sincerity.

**For Lirael:** Don't act young — act old in a young body. She's asking questions she already knows the answer to, because she wants to hear someone else say it.

**For Thorne:** Long pauses are his punctuation. He's edited centuries of thoughts into the fewest possible words. When he speaks quickly, something is very wrong.

**For Korath:** Speak slowly. Each word is a stone being placed. Never rush. Even in combat, his voice is measured — not calm, but certain.

**For Cassian:** Smile in the voice always. Even when threatening. Especially when threatening.

**For Zereth:** Two voices: the corrupted distortion (anger, pain) and the original voice trying to break through (pleading, grief). They overlap. The moments when the original voice wins are the most devastating.
